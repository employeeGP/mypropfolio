# How to Sell Executive Condos as a Singapore Property Agent (2026 Guide)

Selling an EC effectively requires more front-loaded client qualification than almost any other residential segment. The eligibility rules are specific, the 8 May 2026 rule changes have added new complexity, and the financial implications of getting the qualification wrong — a buyer who books a unit and discovers they are ineligible, or who does not account for the MSR constraint — are serious.

The agents who consistently perform in EC sales are the ones who master the qualification conversation first, then build their marketing and presentation approach around a well-qualified buyer pipeline. Here is how to do both.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Qualification — Do This Before Anything Else

### The Six-Point EC Eligibility Checklist

Before taking any EC buyer to a showflat or sharing project information, verify all six eligibility criteria:

**1. Citizenship:** Is at least one applicant a Singapore Citizen? Two PRs cannot purchase an EC. SC + PR is eligible; SC + SC is eligible; PR + PR is not. <cite index="18-1">Two Permanent Residents cannot jointly purchase an EC under any circumstances.</cite>

**2. Income ceiling:** What is the combined gross household income of all applicants? <cite index="16-1">The SGD 16,000 ceiling applies to all co-applicants' combined gross income, including base salary, overtime, bonuses, and commissions. Exceeding this by even a single dollar at the time of application results in automatic disqualification.</cite>

**3. Private property history:** Has any applicant owned or disposed of private property in the past 30 months? <cite index="14-1">This 30-month cooling-off period is absolute — a buyer who sold their private condo 29 months ago remains ineligible.</cite>

**4. HDB ownership:** Does the applicant currently own an HDB flat? If yes, they must sell within 6 months of EC key collection — and their HDB must have already fulfilled MOP before the EC application.

**5. Family nucleus:** Does the application form a recognised family nucleus (married couple, engaged couple, parent-child, orphaned siblings, or joint singles 35+)?

**6. Age:** Are all applicants at least 21 years old? For Joint Singles Scheme, at least 35?

**The 30-month rule is the most common failure point.** Many buyers who approach agents after selling their condo are unaware they cannot apply for an EC for 30 months post-disposal. Catching this early saves everyone time.

### The Income Calculation Conversation

The income ceiling conversation is the most technically complex part of EC pre-qualification, and where agents most often need to dig deeper.

**Variable income earners:** For buyers with commission-based or project-based income, HDB averages the past 12 months. A buyer whose income spikes in one month due to a large commission may need to time their application carefully.

**Dual-income households close to the ceiling:** A couple with combined income of SGD 15,500 today but expecting a pay rise or bonus in the next few months needs to think about application timing carefully. Applying before a salary increment that pushes them above SGD 16,000 — even by a small amount — is critical.

**CPF contributions are not deducted:** The ceiling is gross income, not take-home pay. Buyers who think of their income in net terms need to be walked through the gross calculation.

### The MOP Timing Analysis

Once eligibility is confirmed, the next conversation is about timing relative to any existing HDB flat.

<cite index="18-1">If the buyer currently owns an HDB flat, they can still apply for an EC, but they must dispose of the HDB flat within six months after collecting keys to the EC. This allows them to continue living in their HDB during EC construction — which takes 3-4 years — then sell it just before moving into the EC.</cite>

For an EC buyer who plans to use proceeds from their HDB sale to fund part of the EC purchase, the timeline coordination is important: the HDB sale and EC key collection need to be planned together, not sequentially.

### Old-Rule vs New-Rule EC: Get This Right

<cite index="12-1">EC sites with GLS tender closing dates before 8 May 2026 keep the older 5-year MOP and 10-year privatisation timeline. New EC sites (GLS tender closing from 8 May 2026) have 10-year MOP and 15-year privatisation.</cite>

Before any buyer commits to a specific EC project, confirm which MOP rule applies to that project. This is determined by the GLS tender closing date — not the public launch date, not the showflat opening date.

The four EC projects launching in 2026 (Sora in Punggol, Senja Close in Bukit Panjang, and two others in the north and north-west) were tendered before 8 May 2026 and retain the old 5-year MOP rules. Future EC projects will be subject to the new 10-year MOP.

For buyers who are considering waiting for a future project versus committing to a current one, the MOP difference is a significant factor — and one where your guidance adds genuine value.

## Phase 2: Building Your EC Buyer Pipeline

### Identifying Your EC Target Audience

<cite index="13-1">ECs serve the "sandwiched class" — Singaporeans who earn above the HDB BTO ceiling of SGD 14,000/month but find private condominiums financially out of reach.</cite>

In practical terms, your target EC buyer audience is:

- **HDB upgrader families approaching MOP:** Particularly those in the SGD 10,000-16,000 income range. The EC price point (SGD 1.4-2.2M) is often their most accessible entry into condo living.
- **First-time buyers who missed BTO:** Couples who have been unable to secure a BTO ballot and have incomes too high for HDB but find private condos a stretch.
- **Second-timer families:** Those who have sold their previous HDB and are looking to upgrade, provided they meet the 30-month private property rule.

### Pre-Launch Pipeline Building

EC sales — like all new launch sales — are decided in the weeks before launch day, not on launch weekend. The agents who book the most units are the ones who have pre-registered the most qualified buyers.

**Register interest early:** Most EC developers run VVIP preview phases for agents who have demonstrated qualified buyer interest. Being among the first agents to register buyer interest typically secures priority access to unit selection.

**The pre-registration conversation:** Before registering a buyer's interest, confirm all six eligibility criteria, run the MSR affordability calculation, and confirm the buyer's preferred unit type and floor. A well-prepared pre-registration converts to a booking at significantly higher rates than a speculative interest registration.

## Phase 3: The EC Showflat Presentation

### Lead With Eligibility Context

Unlike a private condo showflat where you can generally assume the buyer is eligible, an EC showflat presentation should begin with a brief eligibility confirmation before any emotional investment is made in specific units.

"Before we go in, let me just confirm your eligibility so we can focus on the right units and pricing. Is [buyer's citizenship status]... has anyone in your household disposed of private property in the past 30 months... what is your combined household income?" — this two-minute conversation prevents a situation where a buyer falls in love with a unit they cannot purchase.

### The MSR Constraint Walk-Through

MSR (Mortgage Servicing Ratio) limits EC mortgage payments to 30% of gross household income — this is often more restrictive than the TDSR for EC buyers.

For a household earning SGD 14,000/month:
- TDSR limit: 55% × SGD 14,000 = SGD 7,700/month maximum total debt
- MSR limit: 30% × SGD 14,000 = SGD 4,200/month maximum EC mortgage payment

At current interest rates (assuming 3.5-4% for bank loans), SGD 4,200/month supports a loan of approximately SGD 870,000-900,000 over 25 years. With 25% downpayment (minimum 5% cash), this supports a maximum purchase price of approximately SGD 1.15-1.2M.

For buyers at the lower end of the income range, the MSR constraint may limit the unit types they can comfortably afford — something to work through explicitly before entering the showflat rather than after.

### The MOP Lifestyle Conversation

<cite index="20-1">For buyers who genuinely intend to stay long-term, the new MOP rules do not represent a fundamental problem.</cite>

The honest showflat conversation about MOP:

"The MOP on this EC is 5 years from TOP [for old-rule projects], which means you are committing to living here from approximately [year] to at least [year + 5]. Based on your family situation, does that timeline work? Where do you see your children being in school, and does this location suit that?"

For old-rule ECs, this is a 5-year conversation. For new-rule ECs, it is a 10-year conversation that deserves significantly more time and exploration of lifestyle fit.

## Phase 4: After Booking — Client Management

### The HDB Sale Coordination

For EC buyers who currently own HDB, the post-booking priority is coordinating the HDB sale timeline. They must sell within 6 months of EC key collection — which means starting the HDB sale process approximately 12-18 months before expected TOP.

As the agent, you can manage both transactions — the EC purchase (done) and the eventual HDB sale (upcoming). This dual-transaction relationship is one of the most valuable client retention opportunities in the EC segment.

### Resale EC Buyers

For buyers who cannot purchase a new EC (income above SGD 16,000, PR couple, 30-month cooling period), resale ECs that have already passed their MOP offer a compelling alternative. <cite index="15-1">Resale ECs have no income ceiling.</cite> They are priced between new launch EC and comparable private condo — offering some of the EC subsidy without the eligibility constraints.

Marketing a resale EC requires a different presentation than a new launch: focus on the remaining privatisation timeline (when will it go fully private?), the current condition and renovation quality, and the specific location advantages that justify the premium over comparable private resale.
