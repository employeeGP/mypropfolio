# Choosing the Right Property Agency in Singapore: ERA vs PropNex vs Huttons vs OrangeTee (2026)

After passing your RES exam, the first major decision is which estate agency to join. This choice shapes your training, your network, your brand positioning, and your day-to-day working environment — often for the first several years of your career.

The instinct is to pick the biggest agency, assuming more brand recognition means more business. The reality is more nuanced: <cite index="31-1">the agency brand matters less than the micro-culture of the specific team you join. In Singapore's real estate world, agencies are divided into teams or divisions. A good mentor will provide on-the-job training, such as tagging you along for actual viewings or teaching you how to handle difficult HDB resale calculations.</cite>

That said, the Big Four agencies — PropNex, ERA, Huttons, and OrangeTee & Tie — each have genuine differentiators that matter for specific career paths. Here is an honest comparison.

---

## The Singapore Agency Landscape in 2026

<cite index="34-1">PropNex continues to dominate as Singapore's largest property agency in 2026, widening its market lead year-on-year. ERA remains second, despite a modest decline in agent count. Huttons records steady growth, maintaining third position. OrangeTee experienced contraction in 2026, with agent numbers declining close to 9%. SRI emerges as one of the fastest growing agencies by percentage.</cite>

In terms of agent count: PropNex (~12,000+), ERA (~8,000), Huttons (~5,700), OrangeTee (~4,000).

Scale alone does not determine which agency is right for you. What matters for a new agent is training quality, team culture, commission structure, technology platform, and strategic fit with your chosen niche.

---

## PropNex: Largest Network, Maximum HDB Reach

<cite index="33-1">PropNex delivers the widest buyer reach in Singapore. As the largest agency with around 12,000 agents, it puts your listing in front of the widest possible pool of buyers — which for a seller means more viewings, more competition, and a better shot at a strong price.</cite>

**Strengths for new agents:**
- Largest internal co-broking network — vital when you do not have your own listings yet, as you can co-broke with experienced agents' listings to build income and experience simultaneously
- <cite index="31-1">PropNex and ERA lead in market share for HDB resale. If you plan to focus on the heartlands, their internal databases and specialised HDB training modules are highly advantageous.</cite>
- Strong brand recognition among mass-market HDB and condo buyers
- Structured training programmes for new agents

**Considerations:**
- With 12,000+ agents, the network is enormous but competition within the agency is also high
- As with all large agencies, quality varies significantly by division and team leader — the PropNex brand alone does not guarantee a good training environment
- Best suited for agents targeting HDB resale and mass-market condo segments

**Best for:** New agents who want maximum reach for HDB resale, or agents targeting high transaction volume in the mass-market residential segment.

---

## ERA: Island-Wide Coverage, Strong Training Culture

<cite index="35-1">ERA Realty Network is the strongest overall choice for buyers and sellers seeking island-wide market coverage, data-driven pricing, and a large, well-trained agent network.</cite>

**Strengths for new agents:**
- <cite index="33-1">ERA is known for island-wide coverage and very high aggregate rating. Its strong training shows in how methodically its better agents price, market, and negotiate a sale.</cite>
- Established training infrastructure and professional development programme
- Strong co-broking culture across the network
- Well-regarded for structured approach to agent development

**Considerations:**
- Second largest agency but currently experiencing modest agent count decline — important context for network vitality
- Like PropNex, best suited for mass-market residential focus rather than specialist segments
- Quality of your immediate team and division leader is more important than the ERA brand

**Best for:** New agents who prioritise a structured training environment and want broad residential coverage from day one.

---

## Huttons: New Launch Specialist, Upgrade Journey Focus

<cite index="33-1">Huttons is the agency most associated with new launches. If you are buying a brand-new condo from a developer, Huttons is one of the biggest project-marketing forces in Singapore, and its agents are often the ones running showflats and holding early-bird previews.</cite>

<cite index="36-1">For sellers who are also upgrading, Huttons' ability to handle both the resale sale and the new launch purchase under one roof is genuinely useful — coordinating the timing so you manage the sale and buy in the right sequence, which matters a great deal given the stamp duty rules.</cite>

**Strengths for new agents:**
- Strongest position in new launch project marketing — if you want to sell new condos, Huttons' developer relationships give you early access to project information and unit selections
- Investment advisory culture — good training ground for agents who want to advise clients on portfolio strategy
- Some agencies absorb initial costs (CEA fees, Professional Indemnity Insurance) as part of recruitment — check Huttons' current recruitment package
- Technology platform including proprietary apps

**Considerations:**
- Approximately 5,700 agents — smaller than PropNex and ERA but a meaningful advantage in new launch
- New launch sales are high-income but high-volatility — fewer launches in a given quarter means less income opportunity
- If you want to focus on HDB resale, Huttons' competitive advantage is less relevant

**Best for:** New agents who want to specialise in new launch sales, or agents who aim to position themselves as upgrade journey specialists (HDB resale + new condo purchase).

---

## OrangeTee & Tie: Data-Driven, Research Culture

<cite index="33-1">OrangeTee is the pick for the data-minded buyer or seller. It has one of the most respected in-house research arms in the Singapore market. Its analysts regularly publish market reports and price trend analysis that even rival agencies quietly read, and that research culture filters down to how its agents advise clients.</cite>

<cite index="35-1">OrangeTee & Tie has built strong new launch marketing and sales practice, consistently ranking among the top agencies by transaction value in new private residential launches. Its research capability enables agents to provide clients with a data-driven assessment of each new launch project, rather than simply presenting developer marketing materials.</cite>

**Strengths for new agents:**
- Research-led culture — agents at OrangeTee are better equipped to give evidence-based market advice, which builds client trust more effectively than generic enthusiasm
- Strong technology platform and digital transaction management tools
- <cite index="34-1">Despite contraction in agent count, OrangeTee remains a recognised name with strengths in research, valuation, and advisory services.</cite>

**Considerations:**
- Currently experiencing agent count contraction — worth understanding why before joining, though smaller team size can mean less internal competition
- Best suited for agents who want to differentiate through market expertise and data-driven advice rather than volume and network size

**Best for:** New agents who want to build a career on market knowledge and analysis, or those targeting the sophisticated buyer/seller segment that values evidence-based advice.

## The Honest Framework for Choosing

After narrowing to 2-3 agencies that fit your niche and goals, the actual decision should be made based on:

**1. The division leader and immediate mentor**

<cite index="31-1">The 'Agency' brand matters less than the 'Division' leader. Review the CEA Public Register to check the transaction history of potential mentors — ensure they are actually active in the niche you want to join.</cite>

A strong mentor at a second-tier agency will accelerate your career faster than a weak team at the largest agency. Always meet the team leader before committing — not just the recruitment presentation.

**2. Commission structure and upfront costs**

<cite index="31-1">Most major agencies operate on a 70/30 or 80/20 split for new agents — you keep 70-80% of gross commission, while the agency takes the remainder.</cite> The split percentage typically improves with tenure and performance milestones.

Ask specifically:
- What is the commission split for my first 12 months?
- Are there performance milestones that improve my split?
- Does the agency absorb CEA registration fees, Professional Indemnity Insurance, and initial portal subscriptions?

**3. Training quality and structure**

Ask to see the specific training programme for new agents. Generic answers ("we have great training") are less useful than: "Here is our structured 90-day new agent programme, here are the modules, here is how the mentorship works."

**4. Team culture and day-to-day environment**

Attend a team meeting as a visitor before joining if possible. The energy and culture of the team you join will affect your motivation and learning speed more than any agency-level branding.
