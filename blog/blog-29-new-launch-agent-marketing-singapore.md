# How to Market a New Launch Condo Effectively as a Singapore Agent (2026)

New launch marketing is won or lost in the weeks before launch day. The agents who are fully booked at launch weekend are not the ones who sent the most messages on Friday — they are the ones who built a qualified buyer pipeline over the preceding 4-6 weeks and nurtured it to the point of readiness.

This guide covers the full marketing playbook for a new launch condo: from initial project research through pre-launch content, buyer pipeline building, and post-launch follow-up.

---

## Why New Launch Marketing Is Different From Resale

In resale marketing, you have a physical property to show — professional photos, a floor plan, a viewing appointment. The product exists and can be experienced directly.

In new launch marketing, you are selling a future product based on:
- A showflat that may not perfectly represent the finished unit
- A site plan and unit mix document
- The developer's track record and reputation
- Location analysis and future growth catalysts
- A financial model of what the investment will look like at TOP and beyond

<cite index="49-1">Content marketing for new launches should address the specific questions buyers have: "Is [Development Name] a good investment?", "Which units have the best views?", "How does [Development Name] compare to nearby condos?" Publishing detailed analyses and guides positions you as the expert on the development and attracts organic search traffic from buyers doing their research.</cite>

The agent who can answer these questions most credibly and most completely will win the buyer's trust — and ultimately their booking.

---

## Phase 1: Build Your Knowledge Base (6-8 Weeks Before Launch)

Before you can market a new launch effectively, you need to know it better than any buyer you will speak to.

**Study the project documents thoroughly:**
- Full site plan with stack orientations and north indicator
- Complete unit mix and pricing matrix (when available)
- Developer's track record — past projects, build quality, delivery timelines
- Surrounding development plans — MRT line extensions, commercial developments, URA master plan changes in the area

**Research the comparable landscape:**
- Recent resale transactions in comparable nearby condos (psf by floor)
- Current resale asking prices for similar unit types in the area
- Rental transactions to support yield projections at TOP
- New launches within 2km that buyers might compare to this project

**Identify the project's genuine strengths and acknowledge its weaknesses:**

Buyers in 2026 research extensively before contacting agents. <cite index="62-1">When these fundamentals align — connectivity, transformation plans, supply dynamics, and buyer demand depth — demand becomes broader and price growth more consistent.</cite> If your analysis is honest, buyers will trust it. If you oversell and they discover weaknesses themselves, they will find a different agent.

## Phase 2: Build Your Buyer Pipeline (4-6 Weeks Before Launch)

### Segment Your Existing Database

Go through your WhatsApp contacts, past clients, and warm leads. For a new OCR launch targeting HDB upgraders, identify:

- HDB owners approaching or past their MOP who have expressed interest in upgrading
- Past buyers who purchased smaller units and may be looking to upgrade
- Investors who have asked about private property yield plays
- Contacts who have enquired about the general area in the past

Create a broadcast list specifically for this launch. Do not send a mass message to your entire database — segment to the people for whom this launch is genuinely relevant.

### Pre-Launch Content Campaign

Start content marketing 4-6 weeks before launch, not the week of. A buyer who has been following your analysis of a project for a month is far more likely to book through you than a buyer who receives their first message two days before launch day.

**Week 4-6 before launch: Area deep-dive content**

<cite index="49-1">Agents posting neighbourhood guides — covering schools, transportation, dining options, parks, and community character — help buyers narrow down location preferences. These become some of the highest-performing content pieces.</cite>

Post a detailed area guide on Instagram, LinkedIn, or TikTok. Cover: MRT access (walking minutes), nearby primary schools (critical for family buyers), upcoming developments, and what the area will look like in 5 years. Tag your business account and include a "DM me for the full project analysis" CTA.

**Week 2-4 before launch: Project-specific content**

Post your project analysis: developer track record, unit types available, indicative price range vs nearby resale, your recommended stacks and floors. This is your most substantive content piece — it demonstrates your depth of knowledge and positions you as the most informed agent for this launch.

**Week 1-2 before launch: VVIP preview registration**

"I have secured VVIP preview access for qualified buyers — this means first look at pricing and priority unit selection before the public launch. WhatsApp me to register before [date]."

This creates genuine urgency (VVIP access is real and valuable) and gives interested buyers a clear action to take.

### The Professional Pre-Launch Information Page

For buyers who want to review the project details before committing to a showflat visit, a dedicated information page makes a significant difference in conversion.

A mypropfolio page for a new launch can house:
- Project overview and key highlights
- Location map with MRT distance marked
- Unit types available with indicative price range
- Your personal analysis of the project's investment case
- Your agent profile and WhatsApp CTA

When a buyer receives this link via WhatsApp, they arrive at a professionally presented page — not a general portal listing page where your project competes with others. This reflects well on you as a prepared, professional agent and gives buyers a single destination for all the information they need to decide whether to visit the showflat.

## Phase 3: Showflat Management and Conversion

### Pre-Visit Qualification

Before a buyer visits the showflat, do a brief 10-minute pre-qualification via WhatsApp or phone:

- What is their timeline? (Are they looking to purchase within the next 3 months, or just exploring?)
- What is their budget range? (Does it match the project's pricing?)
- What is their ABSD position? (SC first purchase, SC second purchase, PR, foreigner — each has different financial implications)
- What is driving their interest? (Investment/yield, own occupation, upgrade from HDB)

This saves everyone's time and allows you to prepare a tailored showflat experience rather than a generic walkthrough.

### During the Showflat

<cite index="64-1">On booking day and the weeks that follow, speed and availability are critical. Be available to answer enquiries, arrange showflat visits, and guide buyers through the purchase process. Agents who combine strong pre-launch marketing with excellent service during the launch period build reputations that drive referrals and repeat business for years.</cite>

Key points to cover with every buyer at the showflat:
- Location walkthrough on the site plan (before entering the showflat unit)
- Stack and floor recommendations based on their priorities
- Price psf breakdown and total quantum for recommended units
- Progressive Payment Scheme cash flow walkthrough
- Exit strategy discussion — what comparable projects have achieved in resale after TOP

### The Objection You Will Hear Most: "I'll Wait and See"

<cite index="67-1">Advantages of new launch include choice of unit (floor, view), newer finishes, modern amenities, and potential early-bird pricing. However, note potential waiting time for completion and movement in market values.</cite>

For buyers who want to "wait and see," the honest response is: "The floor and stack you want may not be available if you wait. Here is what happened at [comparable recent launch] — the best-value units were sold in the first weekend, and the remaining units were either less desirable stacks or priced higher as the developer released later phases."

This is factually accurate for most well-received launches and gives the buyer a concrete reason to act now rather than delaying.

---

## Phase 4: Post-Launch Follow-Up

### The 48-Hour Window After Showflat Visit

Most buying decisions for new launch are made within 48 hours of the showflat visit. Your follow-up in this window determines your conversion rate more than anything else.

24 hours after the visit, send:
- A summary of the units you discussed with specific prices and floor details
- Your recommendation with rationale ("Based on your priorities, I recommend stack X, floor Y — here is why")
- The mypropfolio project page link so they can share with family members for input
- A clear CTA: "I can hold your preferred unit for 24 hours once you confirm — shall I register your interest?"

48 hours after the visit, if no response:
- "Just checking in — have you had a chance to discuss with your family? The [unit type] on stack X is still available. Happy to arrange a second visit or answer any remaining questions."

### Building the Referral Pipeline from Launch Buyers

<cite index="64-1">Agents who combine strong pre-launch marketing with excellent service during the launch period build reputations that drive referrals and repeat business for years.</cite>

A buyer who has just booked a unit they are excited about is in the ideal state to refer friends and family. Ask within the first week of booking: "If you know anyone who was also considering upgrading to private property, I would love to be introduced — I can give them the same detailed analysis I shared with you."

## The Bottom Line

New launch marketing rewards preparation over hustle. The agents who consistently top the leaderboard at launch weekends are not sending the most messages on Friday afternoon — they are the ones who spent the previous 6 weeks building a qualified buyer pipeline, creating the most credible project analysis in the market, and earning the trust of buyers who arrive at the showflat already pre-sold on working with them.

Start earlier. Know the project deeper. Present more professionally. Follow up more specifically.
