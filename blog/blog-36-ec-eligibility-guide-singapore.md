# Executive Condo (EC) Eligibility in Singapore: Complete Guide (2026)

The Executive Condominium is Singapore's most distinctive property type — a hybrid housing scheme that starts as public housing and ends as fully private property. It was designed specifically for the "sandwiched class": households that earn too much for an HDB BTO flat but find fully private condos financially stretching.

<cite index="13-1">ECs were introduced to serve Singaporeans who earn above the HDB BTO income ceiling of SGD 14,000/month but find private condominiums financially out of reach. In 2026, ECs remain one of Singapore's most compelling property purchases for eligible buyers: they offer condominium-standard facilities at prices roughly 15-25% below comparable private condominiums, with the bonus of becoming fully private after a defined holding period.</cite>

But the eligibility rules are detailed and recently updated — with significant rule changes announced on 8 May 2026 that affect future buyers. This guide covers every requirement you need to check before committing to an EC purchase.

---

## The Major 2026 Rule Changes: What Changed on 8 May 2026

Before covering the eligibility requirements, the most critical contextual fact for any 2026 EC buyer is the rule change announced on 8 May 2026.

<cite index="14-1">For EC Government Land Sales sites with tender closing dates on or after 8 May 2026:
- MOP will increase from 5 to 10 years
- Full privatisation will move from 10 to 15 years
- First-timer quota will rise from 70% to 90%
- First-timer priority period will extend from 1 month to 2 years
- Deferred Payment Scheme will no longer be allowed for uncompleted EC units</cite>

**Critically:** <cite index="12-1">The four already-launched 2026 ECs keep the older 5-year MOP and 10-year privatisation timeline. Only new EC sites with GLS tender closing dates from 8 May 2026 onwards are subject to the new rules.</cite>

This means your MOP and privatisation timeline depends entirely on which specific EC project you buy — check the GLS tender closing date, not the public launch date, to determine which rules apply.

## Core Eligibility Requirements

### 1. Citizenship

<cite index="18-1">At least one applicant must be a Singapore Citizen. The family nucleus must include at least two Singapore Citizens, or one Singapore Citizen and one Permanent Resident. Two Permanent Residents cannot jointly purchase an EC under any circumstances.</cite>

This is an absolute rule with no exceptions:
- **SC + SC:** Eligible ✅
- **SC + PR:** Eligible ✅
- **PR + PR:** Not eligible ❌
- **SC + foreigner:** Not eligible ❌ (foreigners cannot purchase new ECs)

For PR couples where neither party holds citizenship, the only EC option is to buy a resale EC that has already passed its 10-year privatisation mark — at which point it functions as fully private property with no citizenship requirements.

### 2. Income Ceiling

<cite index="15-1">The EC income ceiling in Singapore 2026 is SGD 16,000 gross monthly household income. This means all co-applicants' combined gross income cannot exceed SGD 16,000/month. Exceeding this limit by even a single dollar at the time of application will result in automatic disqualification.</cite>

<cite index="16-1">Gross income includes base salary, overtime pay, allowances, bonuses, commissions, and any other taxable employment benefits received in the 12 months preceding the application.</cite>

Key income calculation points:
- HDB averages your last 12 months of income if your earnings fluctuate
- Variable commissions and bonuses are included in the calculation
- If your income is temporarily elevated due to a one-off bonus, timing matters
- The SGD 16,000 ceiling has been unchanged since September 2019 and was not revised in Budget 2026

**What if you exceed the ceiling?** <cite index="15-1">Resale ECs (after their 5-year MOP) have no income ceiling — you can buy these on the open market from the existing owner at market prices. Alternatively, a private condo has no income ceiling.</cite>

### 3. Family Nucleus

ECs are designed for family living. To be eligible, your application must be formed under one of four recognised schemes:

- **Family Scheme:** Married couples, engaged couples, parents with children
- **Fiancé/Fiancée Scheme:** Couple intending to marry (must marry before key collection)
- **Orphan Scheme:** Siblings who have lost both parents
- **Joint Singles Scheme:** Two or more singles aged 35 and above

<cite index="17-1">The Joint Singles Scheme deserves particular attention for single buyers.</cite> Singles under 35 cannot purchase an EC under any scheme. Singles 35 and above can only purchase under the Joint Singles Scheme with at least one other eligible single applicant.

### 4. Age

All applicants must be at least 21 years old. For Joint Singles Scheme applications, all applicants must be at least 35 years old.

### 5. Property Ownership History

This is where many buyers — particularly those who have previously owned private property — are caught out.

**If you currently own private property:** You are not eligible for a new EC. You must dispose of the private property before applying.

**If you previously owned private property:** <cite index="14-1">Any applicant who has owned or disposed of private residential property within the past 30 months before EC application is not eligible. This 30-month cooling-off period is absolute — even if you sold a private property 29 months ago, you remain ineligible for new ECs.</cite>

**If you currently own an HDB flat:** <cite index="18-1">You can still apply for an EC, but you must dispose of the HDB flat within six months after collecting keys to the EC.</cite> This allows you to continue living in your HDB during EC construction (3-4 years), then sell it before moving into the EC.

**If you previously owned HDB:** You must have fulfilled the MOP on your previous HDB flat before applying for an EC.

### 6. First-Timer vs Second-Timer Status

<cite index="17-1">First-time buyers have never owned HDB flats, Executive Condominiums, or DBSS properties. This category enjoys the most favourable EC eligibility terms, including higher CPF housing grants and simpler approval processes.</cite>

Second-time buyers (those who previously owned HDB/EC/DBSS) face additional requirements. Most critically, you must have fulfilled the MOP on your previous public housing before applying.

**Under the new 2026 rules:** <cite index="14-1">The first-timer quota will rise from 70% to 90% for new EC sites under the updated rules</cite> — meaning second-timers will have significantly less allocation in future EC launches. For current projects under the old rules, the 70/30 split between first-timers and second-timers applies.

## Financing an EC: Key Differences from Private Condo

### No HDB Loan — Bank Financing Only

Unlike HDB BTO flats (which offer an HDB concessionary loan), ECs must be financed through a financial institution loan. This means:
- Loan-to-Value (LTV): Maximum 75% for a first housing loan
- Minimum downpayment: 25% (at least 5% must be in cash; remainder can be CPF)
- TDSR: Your total monthly debt obligations cannot exceed 55% of gross household income
- MSR: For ECs, the Mortgage Servicing Ratio caps EC mortgage payments at 30% of gross income — this is the more restrictive constraint for most EC buyers

### CPF Housing Grants

<cite index="13-1">Eligible first-timer EC buyers can receive CPF Housing Grants credited into their CPF Ordinary Account, lowering the effective entry cost. First-timer families are typically eligible for grants of up to SGD 30,000.</cite>

<cite index="16-1">Broader EHG frameworks can extend up to SGD 80,000 depending on income tiers and specific HDB programme alignments.</cite>

Grants are credited to your CPF OA and can be used for the downpayment (the non-cash portion) or monthly mortgage servicing. They do not reduce the cash portion of the downpayment requirement.

### No Deferred Payment Scheme for New-Rule ECs

<cite index="14-1">The Deferred Payment Scheme will no longer be allowed for uncompleted EC units under the new rules (GLS tender closing dates from 8 May 2026).</cite> For ECs under the old rules, DPS may still be available — check with the developer.

## The MOP and Privatisation Timeline

This is the most significant trade-off in any EC purchase — and the area where the 8 May 2026 rule change has the biggest impact.

### Old-Rule ECs (GLS tender before 8 May 2026)
- **MOP: 5 years from TOP** — during MOP, you cannot sell or rent out the entire unit
- **After MOP (year 5+):** Can sell to Singapore Citizens and PRs only
- **After privatisation (year 10+):** Fully privatised — can sell to anyone including foreigners (subject to ABSD rules)

### New-Rule ECs (GLS tender from 8 May 2026)
- **MOP: 10 years from TOP** — longer lock-in period, no selling or renting out the entire unit
- **After MOP (year 10+):** Can sell to Singapore Citizens and PRs
- **After privatisation (year 15+):** Fully privatised — can sell to anyone

<cite index="20-1">The new MOP rules effectively end the "flip after 5 years" EC strategy that many investors used previously. For buyers who genuinely intend to stay long-term, the new measures do not represent a fundamental problem. For buyers who anticipated an exit within 5-7 years, the new rules are a significant constraint.</cite>

## Is an EC Right for You?

The EC makes most sense for buyers who:

✅ Have combined household income between SGD 10,000-16,000/month — the "sweet spot" where EC eligibility applies and the subsidy compared to private is most meaningful

✅ Are first-time buyers — first-timers get higher CPF grant eligibility and priority allocation under both old and new rules

✅ Have a long-term holding horizon — particularly important for new-rule ECs where the 10-year MOP requires genuine long-term commitment

✅ Are Singapore Citizens (at least one applicant) — the citizenship requirement is absolute

✅ Have not owned or disposed of private property in the past 30 months

The EC does not make sense for buyers who:

❌ Need flexibility to sell within 5-10 years (especially new-rule ECs)

❌ Earn above SGD 16,000/month — no EC access for new launches (resale EC is an option)

❌ Have recently disposed of private property (30-month cooling period)

❌ Are both PRs — citizenship requirement cannot be met

## What This Means for Agents Advising EC Buyers

EC eligibility is more complex than condo eligibility — with more failure modes and more opportunity to add value through accurate pre-qualification.

The most common mistakes to prevent:
- Clients who think they qualify but are within 30 months of disposing of private property
- Dual-PR couples who cannot purchase EC under any circumstances
- Clients whose income fluctuates and does not account for bonuses in the ceiling calculation
- Clients who assume all 2026 ECs have the same MOP — they do not

A client who is pre-qualified accurately for EC eligibility, understands the MOP commitment they are making, and has run their financing numbers against both MSR and TDSR constraints is a client who will not face unpleasant surprises post-booking.
