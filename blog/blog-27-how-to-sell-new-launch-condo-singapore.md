# How to Sell New Launch Condos as a Singapore Property Agent (2026 Guide)

Selling a new launch condo and selling a resale property are two fundamentally different jobs. In resale, you are presenting a physical property that a buyer can walk through, touch, and emotionally connect with. In new launch, you are selling a promise — a floor plan, a showflat, and a vision of what will exist in 2-4 years.

The agents who consistently perform in new launch sales are not necessarily the most experienced resale agents. They are the ones who understand what buyers are actually evaluating — and how to guide that evaluation with data, preparation, and professional presentation.

Here is a complete guide to selling new launch condos effectively in Singapore's 2026 market.

---

## Understanding the 2026 New Launch Landscape

<cite index="62-1">Singapore's property market is entering 2026 with a shifting supply landscape. Market projections indicate up to 22 new private residential launches in 2026, offering an estimated 9,700+ units. Supply will be heavily concentrated outside the prime districts, with around 85% of units located in the Rest of Central Region (RCR) and Outside Central Region (OCR).</cite>

<cite index="62-1">The Outside Central Region (OCR) is expected to dominate 2026 supply, making up approximately 64% of total launches — about 55% higher than 2025 levels.</cite> This is driven directly by the HDB MOP supply surge: <cite index="62-1">a 68.9% increase in flats reaching their 5-year MOP in 2026 is strengthening upgrader demand — a key driver supporting OCR new launch absorption.</cite>

Meanwhile, <cite index="62-1">the Core Central Region may see only approximately 1,397 units launched — nearly 47% fewer than in 2025 — reinforcing continued scarcity in prime districts.</cite>

For agents choosing which new launches to focus on, 2026's market dynamics favour OCR projects with strong MRT connectivity, upgrader demand, and clear value proposition versus comparable resale options.

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch Preparation

The work that determines your new launch performance happens before the launch weekend, not during it.

### Know the Project Inside Out

Before you can sell a new launch credibly, you need to understand it comprehensively:

**Developer track record:** <cite index="58-1">Research different condo launches based on location, amenities, developer reputation, and market trends.</cite> Buyers researching a new launch will ask about the developer's past projects — their build quality, delivery timeline, and sales track record. Know the answers before a buyer asks.

**Unit mix and stack analysis:** Understand which stacks have the best orientation, which floors represent the best value, and which unit types are most suited to different buyer profiles (investor vs owner-occupier, family vs couple). <cite index="58-1">Buyers researching how to buy new launch condo Singapore should focus on developer track record, unit facing, and stack selection before committing.</cite> You need to be able to guide this conversation confidently.

**Price psf by floor and stack:** New launch pricing varies significantly by floor and orientation. Know the exact pricing matrix before you speak to any buyer. A buyer who finds you guessing about prices will not book through you.

**Comparable resale transactions nearby:** This is where most new launch agents are underprepared. Every buyer will ask: "Is this priced fairly compared to what's already built nearby?" You need 5-8 recent resale transactions in comparable nearby condos with psf comparisons ready.

**The exit strategy picture:** <cite index="67-1">Always plan how and when buyers will sell — check recent resale volumes in the area and rental yields.</cite> <cite index="61-1">OCR projects with strong connectivity, amenities, and growth catalysts may see sustained demand and price resilience. CCR projects typically generate 2.5-3.0% gross rental yield. RCR projects tend to offer 3.0-3.5%. OCR projects near new MRT stations can achieve 3.5-4.0% gross yield.</cite>

### Build Your Pre-Launch Buyer Pipeline

New launch sales are won in the weeks before launch day, not on launch day itself.

**Register interest early:** Most developers run VVIP previews for agents who have registered substantial buyer interest. The agent who brings the most registered buyers gets priority access to the best units at launch.

**Segment your existing database:** Go through your WhatsApp contacts, past clients, and warm leads. Identify who fits the new launch buyer profile: HDB upgraders approaching MOP, investors looking for yield plays, right-sizers moving from larger to smaller private property.

**Educate before you sell:** Send your pre-registered buyers a pre-launch information pack covering the project highlights, location analysis, indicative pricing context, and the payment schedule. Buyers who arrive at the showflat already informed make faster decisions and have fewer objections.

## Phase 2: The Showflat Experience

The showflat is your primary conversion tool in new launch sales. Managing the showflat visit effectively is where the deal is made or lost.

### Set the Right Expectations Before the Visit

Before a buyer visits the showflat, send them:
- A brief overview of the project (developer, location, indicative price range, unit types)
- A site plan so they can orientate themselves
- The key questions they should have answered during the visit
- Your WhatsApp number for any questions before arrival

Buyers who arrive at a showflat cold — without context — spend the first 20 minutes getting oriented instead of evaluating the units. Buyers who arrive prepared are already partway through their decision process.

### During the Showflat Visit

**Lead with location, not the unit:** Start outside the showflat. Walk the buyer through the site map, point out the nearest MRT entrance, the direction of nearby amenities, the orientation of the block. Context first, product second.

**Guide the unit evaluation systematically:** Show them the showflat in the same order a buyer would live in the unit — entrance, living area, kitchen, bedrooms, bathrooms. Point out the specific design features relevant to their use case (storage for families, workspace nook for professionals, natural light for lifestyle buyers).

**Address the "it's small" objection proactively:** Showflat units are often smaller than buyers expect, particularly for 1 and 2 bedroom units. Address this directly: "The actual unit at [psf] is [X sqm]. Here is how that compares to [nearby resale comparable] which is trading at similar psf — and you get a brand new unit with [developer warranty period]."

**Use the price matrix confidently:** Be specific. "Stack 3, level 10 is priced at SGD X,XXX psf — SGD XXX,XXX total. The view from this stack on level 10 is [description]." Vague answers about pricing destroy buyer confidence in the agent.

## Phase 3: Buyer Qualification and Objection Handling

### ABSD Positioning

<cite index="61-1">For Singapore citizens, ABSD is currently 0% on private residential purchases (first property). PRs face 5% on the first property and 15% on subsequent. Foreigners pay 30% on any private residential acquisition, including new launches.</cite>

Every new launch buyer conversation must include a clear ABSD assessment before the buyer gets emotionally attached to a unit. An SC upgrader who has not yet sold their HDB is in a very different financial position from one who has cash in hand.

### The Payment Schedule

New launch purchases use a Progressive Payment Scheme (PPS). Buyers need to understand the cash flow implications:

- Booking fee: 5% of purchase price (cash, upon OTP exercise)
- Down payment: 20% within 8 weeks (15% can be CPF)
- Progressive payments: Staggered across construction milestones (foundation, framework, etc.)
- Final payment: Upon Temporary Occupation Permit (TOP)

Many buyers underestimate the cash flow demands of PPS compared to a traditional mortgage. Walk through the full payment schedule with every buyer before they book.

### The "Wait for TOP" Objection

Some buyers hesitate because they do not want to pay for a property they cannot occupy for 3-4 years. Address this directly:

"The advantage of buying now at launch pricing is that you lock in the price before completion. Historically, well-located OCR projects with strong fundamentals have seen price appreciation between launch and TOP as the development completes and comparable resale evidence builds. The question is whether the [project name] fundamentals support that trajectory — here is how I would evaluate that."

## Phase 4: Post-Booking Client Management

### The Relationship Does Not End at Booking

New launch sales generate the highest referral rates of any property transaction type — because the 2-4 year wait period is an opportunity for ongoing relationship building that most agents do not use.

**Construction milestone updates:** Send buyers a brief update when each construction milestone is reached (foundation poured, framework up, facade complete). This keeps you connected and relevant throughout the wait period.

**Market value tracking:** Periodically share data on how comparable nearby condos are transacting, reinforcing the buyer's confidence in their purchase decision.

**Pre-TOP checklist:** In the 6 months before TOP, send buyers a pre-handover preparation guide — what to check at the defects inspection, how the handover process works, when they need to arrange their conveyancing lawyer.

**The referral ask:** A buyer who has just booked a unit they are excited about is in the optimal mindset to refer friends and family. Ask naturally: "If you know anyone looking to upgrade or invest and they would benefit from the same analysis, I would be happy to be introduced."
