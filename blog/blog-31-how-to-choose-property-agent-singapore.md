# How to Choose a Property Agent in Singapore: What Buyers and Sellers Should Know (2026)

With over 36,000 registered property agents in Singapore in 2026, finding the right one is not a matter of picking whoever calls first or whoever your neighbour used. The agent you choose will influence your sale price, your timeline, and your stress level through one of the most significant financial transactions of your life.

This guide covers exactly what to look for, what questions to ask, and what to avoid when choosing a property agent in Singapore in 2026.

---

## First: Verify CEA Registration

Before any other consideration, verify that the agent is registered with the Council for Estate Agencies (CEA).

<cite index="51-1">All property agents in Singapore must be registered with CEA through a licenced property agency. CEA's Public Register allows buyers and sellers to check the agent's residential property transactions closed within the last two years, and their disciplinary records within the last two years, if any.</cite>

**How to verify:** Go to the CEA Public Register at app.cea.gov.sg/public-register and search by the agent's name or CEA registration number (format: Rxxxxxx).

This check takes 2 minutes and tells you:
- Whether the agent is currently registered and active
- Their agency affiliation
- Their transaction history (number of transactions in the past 2 years)
- Any disciplinary records

Do not proceed with any agent who cannot provide their CEA registration number, or whose number does not appear in the register.

## What to Look For in an Agent

### 1. Relevant Transaction Experience in Your Specific Segment

This is the most important criterion — and where most people make the wrong comparison.

<cite index="51-1">One key factor to consider is their track record for transactions in the area. Hiring an agent who has completed multiple transactions in the same property type gives you the highest chance of success with your sale.</cite>

The question is not "how many transactions have you closed?" — it is "how many transactions have you closed for [4-room HDB resale in Tampines] in the past 12 months?"

An agent with 200 transactions across Singapore is not necessarily better for your Tampines HDB sale than an agent with 15 transactions specifically in Tampines. Specificity matters more than volume.

**What to ask:**
- "Can you show me 3-5 recent transactions you closed for a similar property in this area, with the transacted prices?"
- "How long did those listings take from first listing to OTP?"
- "What was the final transacted price relative to the initial asking price?"

### 2. A Clear and Professional Marketing Approach

<cite index="51-1">Buyers expect to view detailed listings, video walkthroughs, and neighbourhood guides before scheduling a viewing. The agents who are visible, credible, and accessible across these digital touchpoints capture the lion's share of leads.</cite>

Ask the agent to show you examples of how they have marketed previous listings. Specifically:
- What do their PropertyGuru listings look like? (Professional photography or phone snaps?)
- How do they share listings via WhatsApp? (A portal link or a dedicated listing page?)
- Do they distribute on social media?
- Do they have a buyer network they can reach directly without relying solely on portals?

An agent who presents their listings professionally — with high-quality photography, comprehensive property information, and a branded listing page — is signalling how seriously they take the marketing of every property they represent.

### 3. Honest Valuation

Beware of agents who give you the highest valuation. This is a common tactic called "buying the listing" — telling a seller what they want to hear to win the appointment, then managing expectations down after signing.

<cite index="73-1">Selling a property is a complex process that requires many specialised skills. An experienced real estate agent will be able to accurately determine your sale proceeds, which is important if you are planning to purchase another home.</cite>

A good agent will present a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) showing you recent comparable transactions — the actual prices similar properties in your area have sold for in the past 60-90 days — and give you a realistic price range based on that data.

**The test:** Ask any agent you interview to show you the comparable transactions that support their valuation. An agent who cannot produce specific transaction data to support their recommended price is guessing, not advising.

### 4. Communication Style and Responsiveness

You will be working with this agent for 3-6 months through viewings, negotiations, and paperwork. How they communicate before they have your listing tells you how they will communicate after.

<cite index="51-1">Reviews are essential to assess an agent's reliability. Finding out what other people have experienced will give you insight into their ways of working, their professionalism, and their overall performance.</cite>

**Questions to evaluate:**
- How quickly do they respond to WhatsApp messages?
- Do they ask questions about your situation before presenting their pitch, or do they go straight into talking about themselves?
- Do they confirm the meeting time in advance and show up prepared?

An agent who is slow to respond before they have your business will not suddenly become more responsive once they do.

### 5. Google and Online Presence

After meeting any agent you are seriously considering, Google their name. What do you find?

An agent who appears only on PropertyGuru — with no personal website, no LinkedIn activity, no Google Business Profile — is giving you limited ability to verify their professional reputation independently.

An agent with active LinkedIn content, a well-maintained Google Business Profile with genuine reviews, and a track record visible beyond portal badges is giving you multiple independent signals of how they operate.

## Questions to Ask Before You Sign

Do not sign a Listing Agreement or appoint a buyer's agent without asking these questions:

**For sellers:**
1. Can you show me the comparable transactions that support your recommended price?
2. What does your marketing plan include, and can I see examples of your past listing presentations?
3. How will you communicate with me through the process — how often, through which channel?
4. What happens if the listing does not attract offers within 4 weeks? What will you adjust?
5. What is your commission rate, and what does it cover?

**For buyers:**
1. Can you show me your transaction history for properties in my target area and price range?
2. Do you represent any specific developers or are you fully independent in advising me?
3. How do you search for off-market or pre-listing opportunities beyond what is on the portals?
4. What is the process if I want to make an offer and the seller's agent is from the same agency?

## Red Flags to Watch For

**Overpromising on price.** If an agent's recommended asking price is significantly higher than the comparable transactions you can verify, ask them to justify the difference with data. If they cannot, that is a sign they are competing on price promises rather than genuine market knowledge.

**Vague marketing plans.** "I will list on PropertyGuru and 99.co and see what enquiries come in" is not a marketing plan. Every agent lists on portals. What distinguishes the good ones is what they do beyond the portal.

**Pressure to sign quickly.** An agent who pushes you to sign a listing agreement before you have had time to review it and compare options is prioritising their own timeline over yours.

**No willingness to provide references.** A good agent can provide 2-3 contact details of past clients who are happy to share their experience. If an agent cannot or will not provide this, treat it as a warning sign.

**Multiple properties listed simultaneously without adequate attention.** An agent managing 15-20 active listings simultaneously may not have the capacity to give your property the attention it deserves. Ask how many active listings they currently have.

## The Role of Technology in Agent Selection

In 2026, the way an agent presents listings digitally is an increasingly meaningful signal of their professional standard.

An agent who sends you a PropertyGuru URL when you ask for a listing example is showing you the minimum. An agent who sends you a branded listing microsite — with professional photography, a floor plan, a complete spec sheet, and a direct WhatsApp CTA — is showing you a standard of presentation that translates directly to how your property will be presented to buyers.

Before signing with any agent, ask: "Can you show me an example of what my listing page will look like when you market my property?" The answer will tell you a great deal about the quality of marketing you can expect.

## The Bottom Line

Choosing a property agent in Singapore is not a minor decision. The difference between a well-prepared agent who prices accurately, markets professionally, and communicates clearly, and an average agent who under-prepares, over-promises, and disappears between viewings, can mean tens of thousands of dollars in your final outcome.

Verify CEA registration. Check specific transaction experience in your segment. Ask for the comparable data behind any valuation. Evaluate responsiveness before you sign. And look at how they present their listings — it tells you everything about how they will present yours.
