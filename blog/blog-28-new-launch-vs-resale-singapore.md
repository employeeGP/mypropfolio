# New Launch vs Resale Condo in Singapore: Which Should Your Buyer Choose? (2026)

One of the most common questions Singapore property buyers ask their agent is whether to buy a new launch or a resale condo. It is also one of the questions where agents most often give a generic answer — "it depends on your needs" — without actually walking the buyer through a structured comparison.

This guide gives you the framework to have this conversation properly: what each option offers, where each falls short, and how to guide different buyer profiles toward the right decision based on their specific circumstances in 2026.

---

## The State of Both Markets in 2026

**New launch:** <cite index="62-1">Market projections indicate up to 22 new private residential launches in 2026, offering an estimated 9,700+ units. Supply is heavily concentrated in the OCR, with approximately 64% of total launches outside the central districts.</cite> Key 2026 launches include Tengah Garden Residences (853 of 863 units sold at SGD 2,120 psf on launch weekend), Hudson Place Residences in RCR, and multiple upcoming CCR luxury projects.

**Private resale:** <cite index="57-1">The URA private residential property price index increased by 0.5% in Q2 2026 after 0.9% growth in Q1, with CCR surging 2.0% while RCR declined 1.4% and OCR fell 0.2%. Total private resale transaction volume in Q2 2026 was approximately 5,420 units.</cite>

The divergence between new launch and resale performance is meaningful in 2026: well-priced new launches with strong fundamentals are absorbing quickly (Tengah sold 99% at launch), while resale in RCR and parts of OCR is facing price softening.

## New Launch: The Case For and Against

### Advantages of New Launch

**Buy at today's price, move in later.** If the project fundamentals are strong — good location, reputable developer, well-priced relative to nearby resale — a buyer who locks in at launch pricing may benefit from capital appreciation between launch and TOP. <cite index="61-1">OCR projects with strong connectivity and transformation plans may offer stronger rental resilience and future capital appreciation.</cite>

**Choice of unit.** Buying at launch allows the buyer to choose their preferred floor, stack, and orientation. Resale buyers take what is available.

**Brand new condition with developer warranty.** New launch buyers get a defects liability period during which the developer is responsible for rectifying defects. Resale buyers purchase the unit as-is.

**Modern design and facilities.** Newer developments reflect current design standards — smart home features, larger common facilities, more environmentally sustainable construction.

**Potential progressive payment advantage.** The Progressive Payment Scheme means buyers do not need to service the full mortgage immediately. Cash flow is staggered across construction milestones, which can be advantageous for buyers with incoming liquidity events (bonus, asset sale, etc.).

### Disadvantages of New Launch

**No immediate possession.** Singapore new launches typically complete in 3-5 years from launch. <cite index="31-1">Understand the payment schedule for new launches, which is typically staggered over the construction period.</cite> Buyers who need somewhere to live soon cannot wait for a new launch to be built.

**You are buying from a floor plan, not a physical unit.** The showflat is a representation — often enhanced with furniture, lighting, and finishes that differ from the actual unit. Buyers need to read the specifications carefully rather than relying entirely on the showflat experience.

**Typically priced at a premium to nearby resale.** Developers price new launches above comparable nearby resale to reflect the new condition premium. Whether that premium is justified depends on the location, developer, and local market dynamics.

**Market risk during construction.** If the market softens during the 3-5 year construction period, the buyer's paper value may decline before TOP. This is a risk for buyers who are financially stretched or who need to sell shortly after completion.

## Resale: The Case For and Against

### Advantages of Resale

**Immediate possession.** A resale buyer can move in within 8-12 weeks of exercising the OTP. For buyers with an urgent housing need — approaching MOP on their HDB, lease ending on a rental, relocating — this is often the deciding factor.

**What you see is what you get.** Resale buyers can inspect the exact unit, assess the actual view, check the renovation condition, and evaluate the actual layout rather than a showflat interpretation of it.

**Potential value opportunities.** In a market where OCR resale prices are soft (down 0.2% in Q2 2026) and sellers are more willing to negotiate than in a hot market, resale buyers can find value that new launches at fixed developer prices cannot offer.

**Established neighbourhood and facilities.** A buyer purchasing a resale unit in a 10-year-old development already knows exactly what the facilities look like, how the management is run, what the neighbour situation is, and what the neighbourhood has developed into. New launch buyers are buying into an unknown.

**Shorter timeline to rental income.** Investors who want rental yield immediately cannot wait 3-5 years for a new launch to complete. <cite index="61-1">CCR projects currently generate 2.5-3.0% gross rental yield. RCR projects offer 3.0-3.5%. OCR projects near MRT stations can achieve 3.5-4.0%.</cite> These yields are available now from resale purchases.

### Disadvantages of Resale

**Higher immediate cash outlay.** Resale buyers pay the full purchase price immediately upon completion — there is no progressive payment staggering. The 25% down payment (5% cash OTP, 20% cash/CPF within 8 weeks) is required in full.

**Stamp duty on existing valuation.** For HDB upgraders subject to the Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty timing requirements, the stamp duty calculations on resale may differ from new launch.

**Renovation costs.** Older resale units often require renovation before occupation, adding SGD 50,000-150,000+ to the effective purchase cost depending on the extent of work needed.

**Older facilities and shorter remaining lease.** A 15-year-old resale condo has 84 years of leasehold remaining (for a 99-year project) and facilities that may show their age. New launches start at full lease and reflect current design standards.

## Which Buyer Profile Fits Which Option

### HDB Upgrader (Approaching MOP)

For an HDB upgrader who has just reached or is approaching their Minimum Occupation Period, the key variables are timeline and budget.

**New launch is better if:** They can hold their HDB for 1-2 more years, their upgrade budget aligns with current new launch pricing in their target area, and they want to lock in pricing before further supply enters the market.

**Resale is better if:** They need to complete the HDB sale and private purchase within a compressed timeline (6-month window applies if the HDB is sold first), or if the value available in nearby resale represents better fundamentals than current new launch pricing.

**The agent's role:** Present both options side-by-side with specific examples — actual new launch units at current pricing vs comparable resale units at current market value. Let the numbers drive the decision rather than a preference-based recommendation.

### First-Time Private Property Buyer

<cite index="31-1">Understand your eligibility, plan your finances carefully, and choose wisely between new launches and resale condos. Always think long term about exit strategies and rental demand.</cite>

For first-time buyers without a timeline pressure, new launch typically offers more advantages: full choice of unit, modern design, and the capital appreciation potential of buying at pre-completion pricing.

The key risk to address is financial stretch during construction — a buyer who is at the maximum of their affordability at launch pricing may struggle if interest rates rise or their income situation changes during the 3-5 year construction period.

### Property Investor

Investors need to evaluate the decision primarily through rental yield, capital appreciation potential, and liquidity (ease of exit).

For immediate yield, resale wins. For capital appreciation in specific growth corridors, a well-priced new launch may win. <cite index="61-1">Projects positioned near transport nodes, commercial hubs, and growth districts may offer stronger rental resilience and future capital appreciation.</cite>

The investor conversation should always include: What is the 5-year exit strategy? What is the estimated rental yield from TOP? How does this compare to equivalent resale options in the same area?

### Right-Sizer (Downsizing From Larger Private)

For buyers downsizing from a larger property — typically empty-nesters moving from landed or a large condo to a smaller unit — resale generally wins. They want to move immediately, they know exactly what they want from a unit (layout, facilities, management quality), and they often have a specific neighbourhood in mind that may have no new launches.

## How to Present This to Buyers

The most effective way to help a buyer make this decision is not to recommend one option — it is to present specific, comparable examples of both and let the numbers speak.

For an HDB upgrader looking at 2-bedroom units in a specific district, a comparison table might look like:

| Factor | New Launch Option | Resale Option |
|---|---|---|
| Price | SGD 1.2M (SGD 2,100 psf) | SGD 980K (SGD 1,800 psf) |
| Possession | 2029 (est. TOP) | 8-10 weeks |
| Condition | Brand new | 8 years old, well-maintained |
| Lease remaining | 99 years | 91 years |
| Est. rental yield | 3.2% (from 2029) | 3.8% (immediate) |
| Progressive payments | Yes — staggered | No — lump sum |

This comparison — specific to the buyer's actual options, not hypothetical examples — makes the decision concrete rather than abstract.
