# How to Sell Your HDB Flat in Singapore: Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

Selling an HDB flat in Singapore is one of the most common property transactions in the country — but it is also one of the most heavily structured. <cite index="71-1">Selling your HDB flat involves a few key stages: checking your Minimum Occupation Period, registering your Intent to Sell, finding a buyer, sorting out the Option to Purchase, and submitting your HDB resale application. There are specific timeframes for each step, so knowing what comes next saves you from unnecessary stress and delays.</cite>

This guide walks through the full 2026 process from the seller's side — what happens at each stage, how long it takes, what it costs, and the mistakes that catch sellers off guard.

---

## Before You Start: Check Your Eligibility

### Minimum Occupation Period (MOP)

<cite index="73-1">Most HDB flats cannot be sold until the MOP is met. The MOP starts from the date you collect your keys, and only after this period can you sell your flat on the open market.</cite> For most BTO and resale HDB flats, the MOP is 5 years from key collection.

Log in to My Flat Dashboard on the HDB Flat Portal to confirm your MOP fulfillment date.

### Plan Your Next Move Before You Sell

<cite index="77-1">A smooth sale doesn't start when you find a buyer. It starts when you are clear about what comes next. Whether you are upgrading to a condo, right-sizing for retirement, moving in with family, or waiting for a BTO to be completed — your next step will directly affect how you sell.</cite>

This is not just advice — it has real financial consequences. <cite index="69-1">The most expensive mistake HDB sellers make is buying the next property before selling. If you commit to a $2 million private condo while still holding your HDB, you pay 20% ABSD ($400,000) on the new property at the point of purchase. The remission rule lets you claim the $400,000 back if you sell the HDB within six months of the new condo's OTP date. Miss that window and the ABSD becomes permanent.</cite>

### Calculate Your Expected Proceeds

Before listing, understand what you will actually receive in hand after the sale:

**Sale price** minus:
- Outstanding HDB or bank loan (repaid in full from proceeds)
- CPF refund — the principal you used plus accrued interest goes back to your CPF OA
- Agent commission (typically 1-2% of sale price plus GST)
- Legal/conveyancing fees (SGD 1,800-3,000 for HDB-appointed solicitor)
- HDB resale application fee (SGD 40-80 depending on flat type)

<cite index="71-1">More often than not, the cash in hand is considerably reduced. The money doesn't disappear — it goes back to your CPF OA. But many sellers are shocked by how little cash they receive.</cite> Log in to the CPF Mobile app and check your Principal Withdrawal and Accrued Interest to estimate your CPF refund amount before you decide on your asking price.

## Stage 1: Register Your Intent to Sell

**Timeline: Day 1 → Wait 7 days before granting OTP**

<cite index="69-1">The first move is to log into HDB's portal and submit the Intent to Sell. This is a free, mandatory pre-listing step that confirms your eligibility, opens up your CPF refund estimate, and produces your Resale Statement. You must wait at least seven calendar days after the Intent to Sell is registered before you can grant the Option to Purchase to any buyer.</cite>

The Intent to Sell is valid for 12 months. Most sellers register it as soon as they decide to sell, so the 7-day wait has elapsed by the time a buyer appears.

**What the Intent to Sell checks:**
- Your MOP fulfillment
- Ethnic Integration Policy (EIP) quota status — whether your flat can be sold to a buyer of a specific ethnic group given the block's current composition
- Singapore Permanent Resident (SPR) quota status
- Outstanding upgrading costs or HDB loans

## Stage 2: Marketing and Finding a Buyer

**Timeline: Typically 4-10 weeks**

### Pricing Your Flat

<cite index="71-1">Pricing for HDB resale is more transparent than private. HDB publishes recent transacted prices for every block and flat type. Buyers and sellers can both pull the same data. The realistic price band for your unit is whatever the last six similar transactions in your block or adjacent blocks cleared at.</cite>

Check hdb.gov.sg/residential/buying-a-flat/buying-procedure-for-resale-flats/resale-statistics for recent comparable transactions in your estate.

In the 2026 market, <cite index="67-1">listings priced ahead of valuation are sitting longer; price-to-value listings still clear within standard timeframes. Cash-Over-Valuation bidding has compressed substantially in non-mature estates.</cite> Pricing realistically based on data rather than aspiration is the single most important decision you make as a seller.

### Listing Options

**Self-listing:** List directly on HDB's Resale Flat Listing (RFL) service on the HDB Flat Portal. Free. You manage your own viewings, buyer qualification, and negotiations.

**Engaging an agent:** <cite index="71-1">A good agent will run marketing on PropertyGuru, 99.co, and social media for 2-4 weeks, coordinate viewings, and qualify buyers (HFE status, ethnic quota compatibility, financing capability). Typical seller-side commission in 2026 is 1-2% of the transacted price.</cite>

### The HFE Letter — What Sellers Need to Know

<cite index="69-1">Before you can grant any OTP, the buyer must have a valid HDB Flat Eligibility (HFE) letter. The HFE was introduced to consolidate the buyer's eligibility check, loan estimate, and grant entitlement into one document. It is valid for six months. Ask any prospective buyer for their HFE letter before you accept their offer. A buyer without an HFE is not a real buyer. They can apply for one online but it takes around two weeks to issue, and that two weeks is dead time you do not want during a sale window.</cite>

## Stage 3: Option to Purchase (OTP)

**Timeline: 21-day option period after OTP is granted**

Once you and the buyer agree on a price, you grant the OTP.

<cite index="76-1">The OTP period is 21 calendar days from the date the OTP is granted. During this time, the buyer decides whether to exercise the OTP and proceed with the purchase. The buyer pays an Option Fee to secure the OTP — for HDB resale flats, the Option Fee is between SGD 1 and SGD 1,000. The total deposit (Option Fee plus Option Exercise Fee) cannot exceed SGD 5,000. Once you grant the OTP, you cannot sell the flat to another buyer during the Option Period.</cite>

<cite index="71-1">Market practice in 2026 is an SGD 1,000 option fee. If the buyer fails to exercise, you retain the SGD 1,000 option fee. If the buyer does exercise, the sale becomes unconditional.</cite>

At this stage, HDB will also conduct a valuation of your flat. If the valuation comes in below your agreed price, the buyer must pay the shortfall in cash (Cash-Over-Valuation, or COV). In the current market, COV has compressed in most non-mature estates — something sellers should factor into their pricing expectations.

## Stage 4: Resale Application and Completion

**Timeline: ~3 weeks for HDB processing + 8 weeks to completion**

### Submitting the Resale Application

<cite index="76-1">Within 7 days of OTP exercise, both seller and buyer log into the HDB Resale Portal and jointly submit the resale application. HDB then processes the application, targeted at 8 weeks. During this time, HDB will audit your ownership, verify the buyer's eligibility, compute CPF refunds, and arrange the completion appointment.</cite>

<cite index="77-1">HDB will verify the eligibility of both buyer and seller, check all submitted documents, and ensure everything complies with regulations.</cite>

During this period, HDB may also inspect your flat for unauthorised renovations. <cite index="77-1">If any unauthorised works are found, the seller will be required to reinstate the flat back to its original condition at their own cost before the transaction can proceed. HDB is strict on this requirement, and there is usually little room for negotiation once an issue is flagged.</cite>

If you have made any modifications to the flat — removed walls, altered wet areas, or changed plumbing or electrical works — verify these are authorised before the sale process begins. Rectification during an active sale is stressful and can delay completion.

### Completion Appointment

<cite index="76-1">Typically 8 weeks after the resale application, you attend the completion appointment at HDB Hub. Both parties sign the transfer documents, CPF refund is credited to your Ordinary Account, the buyer's loan is disbursed, and you hand over the keys. You must vacate the flat before completion and deliver vacant possession.</cite>

**What you receive at completion:**
- Outstanding loan repaid directly from proceeds
- CPF refund credited to your CPF OA (not available as cash)
- Remaining cash proceeds transferred to your bank account

## The Mistakes That Catch Sellers Off Guard

**1. Underestimating the CPF refund**
Many sellers receive significantly less cash in hand than expected because of CPF accrued interest accumulated over years of ownership. Calculate this before you set your asking price.

**2. Overpricing the flat**
<cite index="69-1">Overpricing leads to extended listing periods and ultimately a lower final transacted price.</cite> Use actual comparable transaction data, not aspirational estimates, to set your listing price.

**3. Accepting a buyer without HFE clearance**
<cite index="69-1">A buyer without an HFE letter is not a real buyer.</cite> Do not invest time in viewings and negotiation with a buyer who cannot confirm their eligibility and financing.

**4. Unauthorised renovations not addressed before listing**
Discover and resolve any unauthorised works before you begin the sale process — not after an HDB inspection flags them mid-transaction.

**5. Not planning the next move early enough**
The timeline between listing and completion is 3-4 months. If you are upgrading to private property, the timing between your HDB sale and your next purchase needs careful coordination to avoid ABSD exposure.
