# How Much Do Singapore Property Agents Earn? (Realistic 2026 Breakdown)

The property agent career in Singapore has extremes that are genuinely wide apart. Top performers — the agents who close SGD 50-100M+ in transactions annually — earn income that puts them among Singapore's highest earners in any profession. Entry-level agents in their first year often earn less than they expected, with some earning very little until their pipeline builds.

Understanding the realistic income picture — not the aspirational top-end, and not the pessimistic bottom — is essential for anyone considering this career. This guide breaks down the commission structure, realistic earnings by experience level, the costs most aspiring agents underestimate, and what actually drives the income gap between agents at the same agency.

---

## How Property Agent Commission Works in Singapore

Property agents earn commission on closed transactions, not salary. There is no base pay — income is entirely dependent on completing deals.

### Commission Rates by Transaction Type

**HDB Resale — Seller Side:**
Standard commission is 1-2% of the transacted price. On a SGD 650,000 HDB flat, the seller's agent earns SGD 6,500-13,000 gross commission.

**HDB Resale — Buyer Side:**
Traditionally, HDB buyers did not pay agent commission — the seller's commission covered both sides through co-broking. The industry is moving toward explicit buyer representation with separate buyer's commission, but current practice still varies significantly.

**Private Property — Seller Side:**
Standard commission is 1-2% of transacted price. On a SGD 2M condo, the listing agent earns SGD 20,000-40,000 gross.

**Private Property — Buyer Side:**
1% of transacted price is the most common buyer's agent commission for private property. On a SGD 2M purchase, SGD 20,000 gross.

**New Launch — Buyer's Agent:**
New launch commissions are paid by the developer and are typically 2-3% of purchase price, sometimes higher for specific projects during the launch period. A SGD 1.8M new launch unit at 2.5% = SGD 45,000 gross commission to the buyer's agent. This is why new launch is attractive income-wise, despite the boom-and-bust nature of launch cycles.

**Rental — Landlord Side:**
Typically 1 month's rental for leases of 2 years or more. On a SGD 4,000/month condo rental, the landlord's agent earns SGD 4,000 gross.

**Rental — Tenant Side:**
Traditionally half a month's rent for 2-year leases, paid by the tenant. This is evolving and varies by negotiation.

## The Agency Split: What You Actually Keep

Gross commission is split between the agent and the agency. <cite index="31-1">Most major agencies in Singapore operate on a 70/30 or 80/20 split for new agents — meaning you keep 70-80% of the gross commission while the agency takes the remainder.</cite>

Commission split typically improves with performance and tenure:
- New agents: 70/30 or 75/25
- Agents with SGD 1-3M annual GCI (Gross Commission Income): 80/20 to 85/15
- Top performers: 90/10 or higher, with some senior team leaders achieving 95/5 or full 100% (with different fee structures for team benefits)

**What the agency's cut covers:** Their brand, training infrastructure, compliance management, professional indemnity insurance (in some cases), CEA licence oversight, and co-broking database access.

## Realistic Income by Experience Level

### Year 1: The Most Variable Year

<cite index="31-1">Realistic income for a first-year agent varies wildly. While top achievers can earn six figures, the median income for new agents often ranges between SGD 30,000 to SGD 50,000 as they build their pipeline.</cite>

This range reflects the distribution:
- **Bottom quartile (struggling):** SGD 0-20,000. Often agents who could not close their first transaction in 12 months, or who did not invest in building their pipeline consistently.
- **Median (building):** SGD 30,000-50,000. 3-6 transactions in year one, typically a mix of rental and 1-2 resale transactions from personal network.
- **Top quartile (strong start):** SGD 80,000-150,000. Agents who entered with a strong personal network, received excellent mentorship, or landed a few new launch transactions early.

**The honest picture:** Many aspiring agents expect to be in the top quartile based on their confidence and past career success. The actual distribution tells a different story — first-year median is closer to the income of a junior salaried employee than to the aspiration.

### Years 2-5: The Development Phase

By year 3-5, most agents who are still active (many leave the profession in year 1-2) have built a referral base and consistent transaction pipeline. Median earnings for agents with 3-5 years of experience are typically SGD 80,000-150,000/year.

A 5-year agent who specialises in HDB resale in 2-3 estates, closes 15-25 transactions per year, and maintains strong client relationships can earn SGD 120,000-200,000 in a normal market year.

### The Top 10-20%: Where the Large Numbers Are

The agents who earn SGD 300,000-1M+ per year are typically:
- Specialists in high-value segments (prime condo, landed, commercial)
- High-volume agents with strong systematic marketing and referral pipelines
- New launch top producers who close multiple units per launch
- Team leaders who earn overriding commissions on their team's transactions

These income levels are real — but they are not the median experience.

## The Costs Most Aspiring Agents Underestimate

This is where income expectations most often collide with reality. Property agents are self-employed independent contractors. All business costs come from your commission income before you see any net pay.

**Annual fixed costs:**
- CEA registration renewal: ~SGD 350/year
- Professional Indemnity Insurance: ~SGD 300/year (may be covered by agency)
- Mandatory CPD training hours: cost varies, ~SGD 200-500/year

**Marketing and listing costs (monthly):**
- PropertyGuru subscription: SGD 400-1,000/month depending on plan and number of featured listings
- 99.co subscription: SGD 200-500/month
- mypropfolio: affordable monthly subscription for professional listing microsites
- Property photography: SGD 200-400 per listing
- Social media advertising (optional): SGD 300-1,000/month

**Other business costs:**
- Business cards, branding materials: SGD 200-500 initially
- Transport: property agents drive significant mileage for viewings and listing appointments
- Mobile phone plan: data-heavy usage for photos, videos, WhatsApp

**Total annual business costs for an active agent:** Typically SGD 15,000-35,000/year before earning a cent of net income.

<cite index="31-1">Remember that you are an independent contractor; you must factor in costs for marketing (SGD 500-2,000/month), the annual CEA registration fee, and mandatory CPD credits.</cite>

An agent who earns SGD 80,000 in gross commission, at an 80/20 split, nets SGD 64,000 from the agency. After SGD 20,000 in business costs, net income is approximately SGD 44,000. This is a very different picture from the SGD 80,000 gross headline.

## What Actually Drives the Income Gap

Between two agents at the same agency, the same experience level, in the same market — some earn 5-10x more than others. The factors that explain most of this gap:

**1. Niche depth vs breadth**

Agents who own a clearly defined niche — "the HDB specialist for Bishan, Toa Payoh, and Ang Mo Kio" — generate referrals and repeat business far more efficiently than agents who take every type of transaction in every area. The depth of knowledge and the word-of-mouth effect within a niche compound over time.

**2. Systematic follow-up**

<cite index="40-1">Generating a lead is only 10% of the battle. The other 90% is the follow-up process.</cite> Agents who consistently follow up with warm leads, stay in touch with past clients, and maintain an active broadcast programme generate significantly more transactions from the same amount of marketing spend.

**3. Listing quality and presentation**

In a competitive market, the agents whose listings get more viewings and more enquiries are not always the ones with the best properties — they are the ones who present their properties most professionally. Professional photography, a dedicated listing microsite, comprehensive property information, and a WhatsApp CTA as the primary contact mechanism all contribute to higher conversion rates per listing.

**4. Speed of response**

Enquiry response time is one of the highest-impact variables in converting leads to transactions. Agents who respond to enquiries within 15 minutes convert significantly more leads than agents who respond the next day.

**5. Referral system**

The agents who earn the most in years 3-5+ are not the ones who spend the most on advertising — they are the ones who have built a systematic referral engine from their past clients. One referred client costs almost nothing to acquire and converts at dramatically higher rates than any cold lead.

## Is the Property Agent Career Right for You?

The income potential is genuine — but so is the uncertainty, especially in the first 1-3 years. A realistic self-assessment before entering the profession:

**You are well-suited to property agent career if:**
- You can sustain financially for 6-12 months without significant income
- You are comfortable with commission-only income and income variability
- You have an existing network (personal, professional, community) that you can approach for your first transactions
- You are disciplined enough to be self-directed without a boss or structured schedule
- You are genuinely interested in property — the knowledge gap between entry-level and expert is large, and you will need to enjoy learning about the market

**You should think carefully if:**
- You need predictable monthly income immediately
- You are banking on being in the top 10% in year one
- You are not prepared to invest SGD 15,000-20,000 in your first year before seeing significant returns
- You do not have a specific niche or area where you have credibility and relationships to build from
