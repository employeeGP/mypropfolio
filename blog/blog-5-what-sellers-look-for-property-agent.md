# What Sellers Look for When Choosing a Property Agent in Singapore (2026)

Most property agents prepare for listing appointments by thinking about what they want to say. The agents who consistently win listings prepare by thinking about what sellers want to hear — which is a fundamentally different exercise.

Understanding what Singapore sellers are actually prioritising when they choose an agent in 2026 gives you a clear and repeatable edge. This guide breaks it down.

---

## The Seller's Perspective: What Is at Stake

Selling a property in Singapore is one of the most significant financial decisions a household makes. For an HDB flat transacting at SGD 600,000, or a condo at SGD 1.5 million, the seller is trusting an agent with a transaction that may represent decades of savings.

<cite index="24-1">Selling a property is a complex process that requires many specialised skills — valuation, photography, copywriting, advertising, negotiating. It is not possible for a single individual agent to excel in every single skill.</cite>

This is why sellers do not just look for the friendliest agent, or the one with the most impressive branding. They look for the one who gives them the most confidence that their property will sell — at the right price, in the right timeframe, with minimal stress.

<cite index="19-1">With approximately 35,000 licensed real estate salespersons registered with the CEA as at 2026, the quality and suitability of agents varies widely.</cite> Sellers know this. They are actively looking for ways to differentiate between agents who seem similar on the surface.

---

## What Singapore Sellers Actually Prioritise in 2026

### 1. Verified Track Record in Their Specific Property Type and Area

This is consistently the number one factor — and it is the area where most agents present their credentials too broadly.

<cite index="24-1">One key factor to consider is their track record for transactions in the seller's area. Hiring an agent who has completed multiple transactions in the same property type gives you the highest chance of success with your sale.</cite>

A seller with a 4-room HDB flat in Tampines does not find it reassuring to hear "I have closed over 50 transactions across Singapore." They want to know: how many HDB resale transactions have you closed in Tampines? What were the transacted prices? How long did they take?

The more specific your track record evidence, the more credible you become. Generic volume numbers do not move sellers — specific, verifiable outcomes do.

**What this means for you:** Prepare a transaction summary tailored to each seller's property type and district before every listing appointment. Even 3-5 specific, recent examples with transacted prices and days on market are more persuasive than a large aggregate number.

### 2. A Clear and Professional Marketing Strategy

<cite index="17-1">Homeowners looking to sell research agents online before engaging one. Buyers expect to view detailed listings, video walkthroughs, and neighbourhood guides before scheduling a viewing.</cite>

Sellers in 2026 know that the quality of their listing's online presentation directly affects how many buyers see it and how many enquiries it generates. When they are evaluating agents, they are evaluating marketing strategies.

The weakest answer to "how will you market my property?" is a vague description of what you will do. The strongest answer is showing the seller exactly what their listing will look like — with real examples.

This is where a dedicated listing microsite becomes a powerful differentiator. When you can show a seller a live mypropfolio example — a branded page with professional photography, full property specs, floor plan, and a direct WhatsApp CTA — and tell them "this is exactly what your listing will look like," you are making the marketing strategy tangible in a way that words cannot match.

<cite index="25-1">The difference comes when you actually show your marketing instead of simply talking about it. Visual examples leave a lasting impression long after you have left the appointment.</cite>

### 3. Honest and Accurate Valuation

Sellers want to hear a high number — but they trust agents who give them an honest one.

The temptation for agents is to price high to win the listing, then manage expectations down after signing. Experienced sellers know this game, and it damages trust immediately when they figure it out.

<cite index="26-1">An experienced real estate agent will be able to accurately determine your sale proceeds, which is important if you are planning to purchase another home. Your agent can also figure out all your timeline options.</cite>

Presenting a well-researched Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) — with recent comparable transactions, current competition on the market, and a realistic price range — signals competence and honesty simultaneously. If your number is lower than the seller's expectation, explain why with data. Sellers respect agents who can defend their valuation with evidence more than agents who simply agree with whatever the seller says.

### 4. Professionalism and Reliability

<cite index="24-1">Reviews are essential to assess an agent's reliability. Finding out what other people have experienced will give you an insight into their ways of working, their professionalism and their overall performance.</cite>

In practical terms, this means sellers are looking for:

- **Responsiveness** — do you reply to WhatsApp messages promptly? Did you confirm the listing appointment in advance?
- **Preparation** — did you do your homework on the property and the area before arriving?
- **Follow-through** — do you do what you say you will do, when you say you will do it?

These seem like table stakes, but they are where most agents lose sellers' confidence. Showing up to a listing appointment without a CMA, without knowing the property's recent transaction history, or without a clear marketing plan signals that reliability will be a problem throughout the transaction.

### 5. CEA Registration and Clean Disciplinary Record

<cite index="23-1">All property agents in Singapore must be registered with CEA through a licenced property agency. CEA's Public Register allows sellers to check the agent's residential property transactions closed within the last two years, and their disciplinary records within the last two years, if any.</cite>

Savvy sellers check this. Make it easy for them — include your CEA registration number in your pre-listing pack and your marketing materials. An agent who proactively surfaces this information signals transparency. An agent who never mentions it leaves the seller to wonder.

### 6. Chemistry and Communication Style

<cite index="24-1">Trust is the foundation of any relationship with a property agent. To assess if there is personal and professional chemistry, we recommend meeting the agent in person.</cite>

This is the factor that is hardest to manufacture and easiest to underestimate. A seller is about to spend 3-6 months working closely with their agent — through viewings, negotiations, paperwork, and potentially difficult conversations about price reductions. They want someone they feel comfortable communicating with.

This does not mean you need to be the most outgoing agent in the room. It means you need to listen well, ask good questions, and make the seller feel heard rather than pitched at. The agents who start the listing appointment with "let me tell you about myself" consistently lose to agents who start with "tell me more about your situation and what you are hoping to achieve."

## The Gap Most Agents Miss

Most agents focus almost entirely on their own credentials during a listing appointment — their years of experience, their transaction volume, their marketing services. These are important, but they address what the agent brings to the table, not what the seller needs from the transaction.

The agents who win listings most consistently spend the first half of the appointment asking questions and listening — understanding the seller's timeline, their financial situation, their concerns, their ideal buyer profile. They tailor their pitch to what they have learned.

A seller who feels understood is far more likely to list with you than a seller who has just sat through a polished presentation about an agent's career highlights.

---

## A Simple Framework to Apply at Your Next Appointment

Before the appointment: research the seller's property, prepare a tailored CMA, and send a pre-listing pack that addresses all five priorities above — track record, marketing strategy, valuation methodology, credentials, and communication approach.

At the appointment: spend the first 10-15 minutes asking questions about the seller's situation, goals, and concerns. Then present your marketing strategy with real examples — not descriptions — of how their listing will look and where it will be seen.

After the appointment: follow up with your finalised CMA, a sample listing microsite link, and a personal note referencing something specific from the conversation.

This framework consistently outperforms the standard "show up and pitch" approach — because it addresses what sellers are actually looking for, not what agents assume they want to hear.
