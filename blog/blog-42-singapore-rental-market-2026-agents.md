# Singapore Rental Market 2026: What Property Agents Need to Know

Singapore's rental market entered 2026 in a distinctive position: prices at or near historic highs, but with clear signals of stabilisation after two exceptional years of post-COVID surge. For agents advising landlords on pricing strategy and tenants on what they should expect to pay, understanding the current data — by segment, by region, and by direction of travel — is essential.

---

## The Headline Numbers (Q1-Q2 2026)

<cite index="42-1">Singapore's private residential rental index rose 0.3% in Q1 2026 (URA), recovering from a 0.5% dip in Q4 2025, but remains below the 2023 peak. HDB rental index eased 0.1% in Q1 2026, continuing a gradual softening from the 2023 high after two years of elevated rents.</cite>

<cite index="48-1">Condo and HDB rents continued to inch upwards through March 2026, but the pace of growth remained measured. This steady increase pushed the Condo Rental Price Index (SPI) to a record high of 145 in March 2026 — reflecting how rents have built up over time while the pace of growth has slowed, pointing to a market levelling off rather than overheating.</cite>

By May 2026: <cite index="45-1">Condo rents dipped slightly by 0.6% month-on-month in May, after hitting an all-time high in April 2026. This decline was observed across all market segments — CCR fell 0.4%, RCR fell 0.6%, and OCR fell 1%.</cite>

**Median rents Q1 2026:**
- <cite index="42-1">HDB 4-room: SGD 2,600/month</cite>
- <cite index="42-1">Condo 2-bedroom OCR: SGD 3,600/month</cite>
- <cite index="42-1">Condo 3-bedroom: SGD 5,200/month</cite>

**Year-on-year movement (May 2026):**
<cite index="45-1">Overall condo rents are up 1.9% year-on-year, with the CCR leading the growth at 2.8% due to resilient demand from high-income expatriates.</cite>

## The Bifurcation: Two Very Different Markets

The most important insight in Singapore's 2026 rental market is the divergence between segments — the aggregate numbers mask very different conditions in different parts of the market.

### CCR (Core Central Region): Resilient, Expat-Driven

<cite index="42-1">Premium private rents in the CCR are proving stickier, supported by a resilient pool of senior expatriate tenants who cannot or will not rent HDB.</cite>

CCR rents are up 2.8% year-on-year — the strongest performing region. The expat professional segment that drives CCR demand is less price-sensitive than other tenant groups, and the supply of quality CCR product has not increased materially despite record-level completions elsewhere.

**CCR gross rental yield: 2.5-3.0%** — lower yield but stronger capital appreciation thesis. For landlords who bought in CCR at 2020-2022 prices, the rental income component remains solid.

### RCR (Rest of Central Region): Balanced

RCR is the most balanced segment in 2026 — not as supply-pressured as OCR, not as premium-priced as CCR. The city-fringe lifestyle proposition attracts a mix of expat tenants and local upgraders in transition. RCR rents eased 0.6% in May but remain 1.5-2% above year-ago levels.

**RCR gross rental yield: 3.0-3.5%** — best risk-reward balance for investors in 2026.

### OCR (Outside Central Region): Most Supply Pressure

OCR has faced the most pressure from the 2024-2025 wave of private completions. <cite index="42-1">Rising supply from 2024-2025 completions is the dominant dampener; landlords must price competitively in 2026.</cite>

OCR rents fell 1.0% in May — the sharpest monthly decline of any region. For OCR landlords, the message is clear: vacancy risk is real in a market with abundant alternatives at similar price points.

**OCR gross rental yield: 3.5-4.0% for well-located units** — highest yield percentage but also most competition.

### HDB Rentals: Gradually Softening

<cite index="42-1">HDB rental index eased 0.1% in Q1 2026, continuing a gradual softening from the 2023 high. Gross rental yields remain attractive for HDB (4.7-5.6%) compared with private condominiums in CCR (2.6%).</cite>

HDB rental demand is structural — it is driven by expats who value cost-effectiveness and mature estate living, local households in transition, and a growing group of renters making deliberate lifestyle choices around flexibility. <cite index="21-1">Many expatriates specifically seek HDB flats due to their cost-effectiveness compared to private condominiums — a preference that sustains rental demand across mature estates like Bishan, Queenstown, and Tiong Bahru.</cite>

<cite index="45-1">Rental activity for public housing moderated in May 2026, with 2,595 flats rented — a 7.7% drop from April. Unlike condos, HDB rental volumes have dipped 3.3% below the five-year average. This drop in volume comes as more local households successfully move into their newly completed BTO homes, so fewer renters are forced to stay in the leasing market for extended periods. Affordability remains the top priority, with 3-room and 4-room flats making up over 70% of all transactions.</cite>

## Who Is Renting in Singapore in 2026?

Understanding the tenant pool shapes how agents advise landlords on positioning and pricing.

<cite index="29-1">Foreign professionals and their families remain the largest renter group in Singapore, making up a significant share of private condo demand — roughly 40-50% of private condo demand. Local movers (households waiting for new homes to complete) account for around 25-30%. Students and early-career singles represent approximately 15-20%.</cite>

**Foreign professionals:** Seek 2-3 bedroom condos in city-fringe or prime areas. <cite index="29-1">Furnished apartments command a SGD 300-500 monthly premium but rent faster because expats prefer move-in-ready units.</cite> The expat housing allowance model means this group is less price-sensitive on the monthly rental but has clear expectations on unit condition and furnishing quality.

**Local upgrader-renters:** HDB households whose BTO is under construction, or private property owners between transactions. Typically need flexible 1-2 year leases. Seek 3-bedroom or larger units, comfortable with either HDB or private, primary concern is location and practical living.

**Students and young professionals:** 3-room HDB or smaller condo units near MRT. Most price-sensitive segment. High tenant turnover.

<cite index="21-1">Singapore's status as a global financial hub continues to attract international talent. With strong economic growth projected at 2.2% in 2026, demand from expatriates remains robust, particularly in finance, technology, and healthcare sectors actively hiring professionals on shorter-term assignments who prefer rental flexibility over purchase commitments.</cite>

## Key Regulations Agents Need to Know

### HDB Rental Rules

<cite index="23-1">HDB whole-flat leases for non-Malaysian foreigners are capped at 2 years per approval. Non-citizens legally residing in Singapore must hold a valid Employment Pass, S Pass, Work Permit, Student Pass, Dependant Pass or Long-Term Social Visit Pass with at least 6 months validity as at the date of application. HDB whole-flat tenants are subject to a Non-Citizen Quota of 8% at neighbourhood level and 11% at block level.</cite>

**Key checks before listing an HDB for rent:**
- Confirm MOP has been fulfilled (5 years from key collection or legal completion)
- Check Non-Citizen Quota status for the specific block — if quota is full, you can only rent to Singapore Citizens or Malaysians
- Minimum rental period: 6 months for whole-unit rental

### Private Condo Rental Rules

<cite index="23-1">For private condos, there is no cap on rental amount or tenure for most leases. Most leases run 1-2 years. One rule to note for short stays: short-term rentals of fewer than 3 months in private residential property are prohibited under the Planning Act and the Housing Agents Act.</cite>

The prohibition on short-term rentals (Airbnb-style arrangements) applies to all private residential property regardless of whether it is a condo or landed home.

## What This Means for Agents in Practice

### For agents advising landlords on pricing

The landlord market in 2026 is shifting from a position of strength (2021-2023: tenants competed for units at asking price) to one of balance (2026: tenants have options and will negotiate). The agents who deliver most value to landlords are the ones who:

**Price accurately from the start:** An overpriced rental listing sits vacant. In a market where comparable units are available at competitive prices, a listing that starts 10-15% above market will typically sit longer and eventually transact at a lower price than if it had been priced correctly from day one.

**Qualify tenants rigorously:** For HDB rentals, the Non-Citizen Quota check is non-negotiable. For private rentals, confirming the tenant's employment pass status, affordability (typically 30-40% of income threshold), and reference checks reduces the risk of difficult tenancy situations for the landlord.

**Manage expectations on yield:** CCR landlords expecting 4-5% yield on current prices will be disappointed. The honest yield conversation — actual achievable rent vs purchase price — is a service most landlords appreciate even when the numbers are lower than expected.

### For agents advising tenants

<cite index="23-1">Expert takeaway: For the first time in years, the pricing power has tilted back toward tenants. Expat living in Singapore and renting a residential property in 2026 means walking into negotiations with real leverage, provided you understand the rules, the districts and the true cost of a lease.</cite>

Agents who help tenants understand the current market — where to find value, how to negotiate, what to check before signing — build the kind of trust that generates referrals and repeat business when the tenant next relocates or purchases.

## The 2H 2026 Rental Outlook

<cite index="45-1">Property experts expect Singapore's rental market to remain relatively stable for the remainder of 2026. While household formation will support demand, a cautious global economic environment may temper rapid growth.</cite>

<cite index="42-1">The key upside risk to the softening thesis is a reversal in Singapore's technology and financial services hiring cycle — any rebound in Employment Pass issuances would tighten rental supply rapidly given low vacancy rates in well-located projects. The key downside risk is continued elevated completions through 2026-2027 from the record launch years of 2021-2022, which will maintain supply pressure on mid-market condominiums.</cite>

For agents, the practical message is: the rental market in 2H 2026 rewards accurate pricing, strong tenant qualification, and professional presentation of every listing. The days of any unit renting at asking price within a week regardless of quality are over — at least temporarily.
