# HDB Resale Market 2026: What Singapore Property Agents Need to Know

Singapore's HDB resale market has entered territory not seen since 2019. <cite index="32-1">The HDB Resale Price Index dipped 0.1% in Q1 2026 — the first quarterly decline in nearly seven years, the last being a 0.2% drop in Q2 2019.</cite> Q2 2026 followed with a further 0.3% decline, confirming a back-to-back softening that changes how agents need to approach conversations with both sellers and buyers.

This is not a collapse. Transaction volumes remain healthy, and the long-term trajectory of HDB prices is still positive. But the dynamics have shifted enough that agents who are still operating on 2023-2024 assumptions — where almost anything sold quickly at or above asking — will find sellers frustrated and listings sitting longer than expected.

Here is what the data shows, and how to adapt.

---

## The Numbers in Context

<cite index="36-1">As of Q1 2026, the HDB Resale Price Index stands at 203.4, down 0.1% from the previous quarter and up 1.2% year-on-year. The median HDB resale price across all flat types is SGD 630,000, with 6,046 resale transactions registered in Q1 2026.</cite>

By Q2 2026, the RPI had slipped a further 0.3% to 202.7.

**Median prices by flat type (Q1 2026):**
- 3-room: <cite index="36-1">SGD 445,000</cite>
- 4-room: <cite index="36-1">SGD 628,000 (up 1.1% year-on-year)</cite>
- 5-room: <cite index="36-1">SGD 745,000</cite>

**Transaction volumes:**
- <cite index="30-1">Q2 2026: 6,268 HDB resale flats transacted</cite>
- <cite index="30-1">1H 2026 total: 12,553 units — 8.3% lower year-over-year than the 13,692 units sold in 1H 2025</cite>
- Full year 2026 forecast: <cite index="30-1">ERA and PropNex both project 26,000 to 27,000 transactions</cite>

**Million-dollar flats:**
<cite index="30-1">491 million-dollar flat transactions were recorded in Q2 2026, bringing 1H 2026's tally to 902 deals — surpassing the 763 recorded in 1H 2025.</cite> The premium segment is performing differently from the mass market — an important nuance for how agents position individual listings.

---

## Why Is This Happening Now?

Three structural factors are converging simultaneously:

**1. The MOP supply surge**

<cite index="30-1">A total of 13,480 units are set to reach MOP in 2026, 93.3% more than the 6,973 units in 2025.</cite> Nearly double the supply of newly eligible resale flats entering the market at the same time is the single biggest driver of price moderation.

<cite index="30-1">With this fresh and attractive supply available, resale HDB activity has naturally picked up. These younger MOP flats, with over 90 years of lease remaining, are highly sought-after as they offer buyers 'new' homes without the three- to five-year wait.</cite>

For sellers of older resale flats — particularly 15-20 year old units in non-mature estates — this creates direct competition from much newer stock at similar prices.

**2. BTO pipeline expansion**

<cite index="30-1">Non-urgent buyers prioritising affordability may pivot toward the BTO market, especially with popular locations being offered. The upcoming October BTO exercise, the largest of the year, will feature 7,970 units across six locations, including standout projects at Toa Payoh West and Bayshore.</cite>

Buyers without urgent timelines are increasingly choosing BTO over resale — reducing the pool of active resale buyers.

**3. Cooling measures still binding**

<cite index="30-1">Cooling measures such as the 15-month wait-out period for private property sellers have eased pressure on resale HDB prices since the start of 2025.</cite> Former private property owners who might otherwise have added demand to the resale market are constrained from doing so immediately.

---

## The Split Market: What It Means in Practice

The aggregate index numbers hide a meaningful divergence within HDB resale:

**Mature estates and premium units:** Million-dollar flat transactions are at record highs. Bukit Merah, Toa Payoh, Queenstown, and Bishan continue to command strong premiums for well-located, high-floor units. <cite index="32-1">Million-dollar flats kept rising while standard 4-room and 3-room mass-market resale eased.</cite>

**Non-mature estates and older stock:** This is where the softening is most pronounced. <cite index="32-1">Cash-Over-Valuation bidding has compressed substantially in non-mature estates.</cite> Buyers in Woodlands, Jurong, Punggol, and Sengkang have more options from newly MOP-eligible flats and are less willing to pay significant premiums for older units.

**What this means for agent conversations:**

A seller in Toa Payoh with a high-floor unit and a recent renovation is in a different market than a seller in Woodlands with a 2005-built unit. Both may be looking at similar headline price index numbers, but their actual transaction environment is very different. Agent credibility depends on being able to make this distinction clearly and base your valuation on genuinely comparable transactions rather than the aggregate index.

## How to Position Listings in a Softening HDB Market

**Price to the actual market, not the peak**

<cite index="32-1">For sellers, the message is to price realistically. The Q1 2026 evidence is that listings priced ahead of valuation are sitting longer; price-to-value listings still clear within standard timeframes.</cite>

The conversation with a seller who has aspirational price expectations needs to be data-driven: show them the actual comparable transactions from the past 60-90 days, not the peak transactions from 2022-2023. The market has moved, and pricing as if it has not is the fastest way to create an extended listing with frustrated sellers.

**Lean harder on presentation quality**

In a hot market, buyers are willing to overlook imperfect photos or sparse listing information because urgency drives decisions. In a buyer's market with more options, the quality of how you present the property becomes a more significant factor in whether a buyer bothers to enquire at all.

Professional photography, a comprehensive listing page with full specs and floor plan, and a professional agent profile attached to every listing are more important in a softening market than in a strong one — because buyers have the luxury of being selective about which listings to pursue.

**Help sellers understand the MOP supply context**

Sellers who understand why there is more competition now are less likely to blame their agent for a slower sale. Proactively educating sellers about the MOP supply surge — and how it specifically affects their unit's competitive position — is both honest and builds trust.

"Your flat is competing with X newly MOP-eligible units in this estate this year. Here is how we position it to stand out from that supply" is a more credible conversation than "the market is a bit slower right now."

**Time the conversation about upgrading carefully**

<cite index="45-1">If your flat is approaching the end of its minimum occupation period, the timing decisions ahead are critical.</cite> For sellers who plan to use proceeds from the HDB sale to fund a private upgrade, softer resale prices reduce their effective upgrade budget. Having this conversation honestly — with an accurate view of both their likely sale price and the private market they are entering — helps sellers make decisions they will not regret later.

## Opportunities the Softening Creates

A cooling HDB resale market is not entirely negative for agents. Several opportunities emerge:

**More realistic seller expectations:** Sellers who are motivated to transact are more likely to accept accurate valuations rather than holding out for peak prices — which makes the agent's job of managing the listing more straightforward.

**Buyers with more time to decide:** <cite index="35-1">The gradual increase in MOP supply appears to be giving buyers more options and helping to keep price growth in check. Buyers have more room to negotiate and stress-test their finances rather than chase.</cite> Buyers who are not in a panic are better clients — they transact thoughtfully rather than impulsively, which typically leads to smoother deals.

**Differentiation through market knowledge:** In a hotter market, most agents could generate enquiries without sophisticated market understanding. In a normalising market, agents who can clearly explain what is happening and why — backed by data — stand out from those who simply say "the market is slower."

---

## The 2H 2026 Outlook for HDB

<cite index="30-1">Housing demand remains supported by Singapore's steady domestic growth outlook, with full-year GDP growth expected to hold steady between 2.0 and 4.0 per cent.</cite> The fundamentals are stable. The MOP supply surge is temporary — it peaks in 2026 and normalises in subsequent years. The consensus view from ERA, PropNex, and OrangeTee is that the market stabilises in 2H 2026 as MOP supply is absorbed.

For agents, the message is: adapt your conversations and pricing strategy to the current data, not the 2022-2024 peak. The underlying demand is there. What has changed is how competitive each individual listing is — and that is where professional marketing, accurate pricing, and clear seller communication make the difference.
