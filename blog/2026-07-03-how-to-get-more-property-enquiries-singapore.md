The average Singapore buyer spends less than eight seconds on a listing before deciding whether to enquire or scroll past. Eight seconds. That is not enough time to read your description, check the floor plan, or appreciate your carefully chosen photos.

If your enquiry rate is low, the problem is almost never the property. It is the first impression your listing makes — and how it stacks up against the dozen identical ones sitting above and below it on PropertyGuru or 99.co.

This guide covers what actually drives buyers to hit "enquire" — not theory, but practical changes you can make to your current listings this week.

## Why most listings look identical

Walk through any major Singapore portal today and you will notice a pattern. Every listing has a bright hero photo, a price, a list of amenities, and a short description that starts with "Cosy and well-maintained unit" or "Rare gem in prime location." The format is dictated by the portal. The copy is interchangeable.

Buyers have adapted. They no longer read listings carefully on first pass. They scan for signals: does this look worth my time? If the first photo does not stop them, nothing else gets seen.

The agents who consistently generate enquiries are not necessarily the ones with the best properties. They are the ones who have figured out how to make the first impression count — and how to build enough trust that the buyer picks up the phone.

## Lead with your best photo

Most agents lead with the living room. Buyers expect the living room. If your property has a genuinely standout feature — a pool view, a renovated kitchen, a corner unit with natural light on three sides — lead with that instead.

Your hero photo is doing a single job: getting the buyer to stop scrolling. Choose the one image that makes the property feel worth their time, even before they know the price.

Practical tip: take five test photos from different angles in the best light of day. Show them to someone unfamiliar with the property and ask which one makes them want to walk in. That is your hero photo.

## Write a first line that does actual work

Your first line of description is the only text most buyers will read. "Rare gem near MRT" tells them nothing. Try to answer the real question the buyer has: *why should I spend my Saturday looking at this instead of the other three viewings I have booked?*

A stronger first line gives them a reason:

- "Corner unit with zero facing neighbours — you get natural light from 7am to 4pm."
- "HDB that has been fully renovated to condo standards. The kitchen alone took four months."
- "Unblocked pool view from the master bedroom. The next unit with this view sold last month."

One specific, true detail will outperform five lines of generic copy every time.

## Include the neighbourhood context buyers actually want

Buyers are not just buying a unit. They are buying into a daily routine — the coffee shop downstairs, the walk to the MRT, the school within 1km. Most listings mention the nearest MRT and stop there.

Go deeper. Which exit? How many minutes on foot, not "walking distance"? Is there a wet market, a hawker centre, a childcare centre within five minutes? What does the morning commute look like at 8am?

This information takes 20 minutes to write once, and it answers the questions buyers would otherwise have to WhatsApp you about. When you answer those questions upfront, you screen out time-wasters and attract buyers who have already decided they like the location.

## Make it easy to enquire from any device

This sounds obvious, but check your listing on a mobile phone right now. Is your phone number clickable? Is there a WhatsApp button? Can a buyer message you in under three taps?

Most portal listings require the buyer to copy your number, open WhatsApp, paste it in, and type a message. That is four steps too many. Every extra step loses a percentage of buyers who were interested but not committed enough to bother.

A mypropfolio microsite includes a one-tap WhatsApp button that opens a pre-filled message so buyers can reach you in a single tap. Small friction reduction, measurable impact on contact rates.

## Use social proof

If the property has had viewings, mention the interest level. "Three offers received last week, seller has decided to continue marketing" signals that other buyers see value. If you have a testimonial from a previous landlord or buyer, a single sentence carries more weight than paragraphs of your own copy.

If you have sold or rented similar units in the same development, mention it. "I have handled four transactions in this block in the past two years" tells the buyer you know the development and can answer their specific questions — which makes you easier to trust and harder to ignore.

## Track what is actually working

Most agents do not know where their enquiries come from. They list on two portals, share on social media, and wait. When an enquiry comes in, they have no idea which channel sent it.

If you use a dedicated microsite for each listing, you can track exactly how many visitors the page gets, where they came from, and how long they stayed. That data tells you whether your WhatsApp campaign is working, whether your portal listing is sending traffic, and whether your hero photo is holding attention.

Without that data, you are optimising blind. With it, you can make one change, measure the impact, and improve with each listing.

Getting more enquiries is not about listing on more portals or spending more on boosts. It is about making your listing easier to remember, easier to trust, and easier to contact you from.

The agents who generate the most enquiries are the ones who treat each listing as a marketing piece — not a form to fill in.
