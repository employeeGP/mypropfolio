You uploaded the photos. You wrote the description. You paid for a featured listing slot on PropertyGuru. And now you wait.

One day passes. Three days. A week.

A handful of views, but barely any enquiries — and the ones that do come in are half-hearted, tyre-kickers, or agents doing market research.

If this sounds familiar, the problem almost certainly is not the property itself. Properties sell every day in Singapore. The problem is how the listing is being presented — and where.

Here are the five real reasons your listing is not converting views into enquiries, and what to do about each one.

## Why This Matters More Than Ever in 2026

<cite index="13-1">Property portals like PropertyGuru and 99.co, which once dominated lead generation, are increasingly expensive and competitive. A featured listing on PropertyGuru can cost $200 to $500 per month with no guarantee of enquiries.</cite>

At the same time, <cite index="15-1">property buyers conduct an average of 10 hours of online research before contacting an agent.</cite> That research happens across multiple platforms — portals, Google, social media, WhatsApp referrals. A listing that only exists on one portal, presented in a generic format, is invisible for most of that research journey.

The agents who are consistently getting enquiries in 2026 are not necessarily paying more for ads. They are presenting their listings better — across more touchpoints, with more information, and with clearer calls to action.

## The 5 Real Reasons Your Listing Is Getting Ignored

### Reason 1: Your Photos Are Not Doing the Work

This is the single biggest conversion killer, and it is the most frequently underestimated.

<cite index="12-1">Buyers expect to view detailed listings, video walkthroughs, and neighbourhood guides before scheduling a viewing.</cite> When your listing photos look identical to the other 20 condos on the same page — slightly overexposed, taken with a phone, no staging — there is no visual reason to click through.

Professional property photography is not a luxury. It is the difference between a listing that gets 200 views and 2 enquiries, and one that gets 200 views and 20 enquiries. The photos are your first impression, and buyers make a decision in under 3 seconds whether to read further or scroll past.

**The fix:** Invest in professional photography for every listing you take. If budget is a constraint, prioritise the hero shot (main living area or best exterior angle) — this is the image that determines click-through rate from the portal thumbnail.

### Reason 2: Buyers Cannot Get Answers Without Contacting You

A buyer who has to send an enquiry just to find out the floor plan, the maintenance fee, the tenure, or the nearby schools — is a buyer who will often not bother. <cite index="13-1">Video provides an immersive experience that answers questions before they are asked, qualifying the viewer before they ever contact you.</cite>

The same principle applies to listing pages. The more information you provide upfront — floor plan, PSF breakdown, tenure, nearby amenities, estimated mortgage — the more qualified the enquiries you receive. Buyers who reach out after seeing comprehensive information are further down the decision funnel.

**The fix:** Treat your listing like a pre-sales conversation. Include everything a buyer would ask in a viewing: floor plan, facing direction, storey range, estimated monthly instalment at current rates, walking distance to MRT, and nearby primary schools. A dedicated listing microsite (rather than a portal page with character limits) gives you the space to do this properly.

### Reason 3: There Is No Clear, Low-Friction Way to Contact You

Most portal listings default to an enquiry form or an email. Both require the buyer to fill in fields, wait for a response, and trust that someone will follow up. For a casual browser who is 60% interested, that friction is enough to move on.

In Singapore's mobile-first market, WhatsApp is the default communication tool. <cite index="12-1">The agents who are visible, credible, and accessible across these digital touchpoints capture the lion's share of leads.</cite> If your listing does not have a direct WhatsApp button as the primary CTA, you are adding unnecessary friction at the most critical moment.

**The fix:** Add a WhatsApp CTA to every listing — not just a form. The message can be pre-populated ("Hi, I saw your listing for [property address] and would like to know more") so the buyer does not even have to type. One tap, and they are talking to you.

### Reason 4: Your Listing Cannot Be Shared Effectively

Word-of-mouth and peer referrals are still one of the strongest sources of property enquiries in Singapore. A buyer who sees your listing and thinks "this might suit my colleague" should be able to share it instantly, in a format that looks good when previewed on WhatsApp or Telegram.

A PropertyGuru URL shared on WhatsApp shows a generic portal preview — the listing competes with the portal's branding, not yours. Worse, the link may show as a redirect, stripping out the property details entirely on some devices.

**The fix:** A dedicated listing page with proper Open Graph tags (title, description, and a clean featured image) previews beautifully when shared on any platform. This means every time a buyer shares your listing with their network, it looks professional and compelling — not like a forwarded portal link.

### Reason 5: There Is No Personal Credibility Attached to the Listing

Buyers and sellers do not just evaluate properties — they evaluate agents. When a listing has no indication of who the agent is, what their track record looks like, and why they should be trusted — the enquiry threshold goes up.

<cite index="14-1">In 2026, successful property agents are not just skilled negotiators and market experts — they are also savvy digital marketers who understand how to build a personal brand, generate leads online, and convert digital enquiries into closed transactions.</cite>

A listing that includes the agent's photo, a short bio, their niche specialisation, and 2-3 specific testimonials converts significantly better than an anonymous listing with a phone number.

**The fix:** Attach your personal brand to every listing you publish. This means your photo, a one-liner about your specialisation, and at least one specific client outcome ("Helped a family of 4 upgrade from HDB to condo in Bishan, completed in 6 weeks"). This context is what turns a viewer into a caller.

## What Buyers Actually Want Before They Enquire

Research consistently shows that buyers contact an agent when they feel:

1. **Informed** — they have enough information to know this property is worth a viewing
2. **Qualified** — the property matches their budget, timeline, and requirements
3. **Comfortable** — they feel confident the agent is professional and trustworthy

A generic portal listing addresses maybe one of these. A well-built listing presentation — with comprehensive property information, a clear WhatsApp CTA, and visible agent credibility — addresses all three.

The goal is not to replace the conversation. It is to make the conversation easier to start.

## How Agents Are Fixing This in 2026

The most effective shift Singapore agents are making is moving away from portal-only listing distribution toward a two-layer approach:

**Layer 1 — Portal presence** (for discovery): Continue listing on PropertyGuru and 99.co for top-of-funnel visibility. Optimise photos and headline copy for click-through.

**Layer 2 — Dedicated listing microsite** (for conversion): For every serious listing, create a standalone branded page that hosts the full property presentation — professional photos, floor plan, agent bio, WhatsApp CTA, and shareable link. This is what you send via WhatsApp, post on social media, and share at open houses.

## Quick Wins You Can Apply Today

Not ready to overhaul your entire listing strategy? Start with these three changes this week:

**1. Replace your weakest listing photo.** Identify the one property in your current portfolio with the lowest enquiry rate. Book a professional photographer for the hero shot only. See if enquiry rate changes within 7 days.

**2. Add a WhatsApp link to your next listing.** Use wa.me/[your number]?text=[pre-filled message] as your primary CTA. Track how many people use it versus the portal form.

**3. Send your next listing as a dedicated link, not a portal URL.** Even a simple landing page with photos, specs, and your contact info — shared via WhatsApp — performs better than a portal redirect in peer-to-peer sharing.

## The Bottom Line

If your listing is getting views but not enquiries, the portal algorithm is not the problem. The presentation is.

Buyers are ready to enquire — they just need enough information to feel confident doing so, and a frictionless way to reach you when they are ready. Fix the photos, add the context, put your personal brand front and centre, and make it one tap to start the conversation.
