# How to Create a Property Microsite in Singapore (And Why Agents Are Switching)

A property microsite is a dedicated web page — or small website — built specifically for a single listing. Instead of sending a buyer a PropertyGuru link that looks identical to hundreds of other listings, you send them a branded page that shows your property at its best, with your name and contact details front and centre.

The concept is not new. Property developers in Singapore have been using microsites for new launches for years — dedicated sites for Lentor Hills Residences, Tembusu Grand, and similar projects that give buyers a branded, immersive experience before the showflat even opens.

What is new in 2026 is that individual property agents are now doing the same thing for resale and rental listings — at a fraction of the cost and effort.

---

## Why a Portal Link Is Not Enough in 2026

<cite index="33-1">92% of property buyers in Singapore begin their search online, visiting at least five different property websites before shortlisting options. Buyers who engage with high-quality digital content are 67% more likely to request viewings and move 40% faster through the decision-making process.</cite>

The problem with portal links is not that they do not work for discovery — PropertyGuru and 99.co remain the primary search channels for Singapore buyers. The problem is what happens after discovery.

When a buyer clicks on your PropertyGuru listing:
- They see your listing competing on the same page with 5-10 similar properties
- They cannot share it in a way that looks professional on WhatsApp preview
- The branding belongs to PropertyGuru, not to you as the agent
- Character limits prevent you from including comprehensive property information

A dedicated listing microsite solves all four problems simultaneously.

---

## What a Property Microsite Includes

A well-built property microsite for a Singapore listing typically includes:

**Professional photo gallery** — a full-screen gallery featuring all professional photos, not the compressed thumbnail format of portal listings. Buyers can scroll through high-resolution images without navigating away.

**Complete property information** — floor plan, tenure, storey range, facing direction, PSF, estimated monthly mortgage at current rates, maintenance fees, and nearby amenities. Everything a buyer needs to decide whether to arrange a viewing, on a single page.

**Neighbourhood context** — walking distance to MRT stations, nearby primary schools (critical for families), hawker centres, and shopping malls. This information is what buyers research separately after seeing a listing — having it on the microsite answers the question before it is asked.

**Agent profile and credibility** — your professional headshot, a one-liner about your specialisation, your CEA registration number, and 1-2 specific client testimonials. The buyer knows who they are dealing with before they make contact.

**Direct WhatsApp CTA** — a prominent button that opens a pre-filled WhatsApp message, so enquiring requires one tap rather than filling in a form.

**Shareable link with proper preview** — when the buyer shares the link on WhatsApp or Telegram, the preview shows your property photo and title, not a generic portal thumbnail.

## The Two Ways Singapore Agents Are Creating Microsites

### Option 1: Build One Manually (Not Recommended for Most Agents)

Technically, any agent can create a single-property website using tools like Wix, Squarespace, or WordPress. The challenges:

- Setup takes 4-8 hours per listing, even with templates
- You need to know basic web design to make it look professional
- Ongoing hosting costs apply for each listing ($5-20/month)
- The site needs to be taken down or updated after the listing sells

For most agents managing multiple active listings simultaneously, building microsites manually is not a viable workflow.

## How to Use a Property Microsite Effectively

Creating the microsite is only the first step. The agents who get the most value from dedicated listing pages use them across multiple touchpoints:

**At the listing appointment** — show the seller a live example of what their listing page will look like. This is one of the most powerful ways to demonstrate your marketing approach concretely. <cite index="25-1">The difference comes when you actually show your marketing instead of simply talking about it. Visual examples leave a lasting impression long after you have left the appointment.</cite>

**In your WhatsApp broadcasts** — send your active buyer broadcast list the microsite link, not the portal URL. Buyers who click through see a professional page rather than a portal listing competing with other properties.

**On social media** — share the microsite link on Instagram Stories or Facebook with a brief caption. When followers tap the link, they arrive at a branded page that reflects your professional standard.

**At open houses and viewings** — display the microsite QR code at the entrance so buyers can access the full listing information on their phone during the viewing.

**In your pre-listing pack** — include a sample microsite link as evidence of how you present properties. This addresses the seller's question "how will my listing look online?" with a tangible example rather than a description.
