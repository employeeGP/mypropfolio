# Buying Landed Property in Singapore: What You Need to Know (2026 Guide)

Landed property sits at the top of Singapore's residential hierarchy — scarce, prestigious, and governed by a set of rules that catch many buyers off guard. Whether you are a Singapore Citizen planning your first landed purchase, a Permanent Resident exploring your options, or an agent advising a client on their upgrade journey, understanding the eligibility framework, cost structure, and buying process is essential before any serious search begins.

This guide covers the full picture for 2026.

---

## What Counts as "Landed Property" in Singapore

The term landed property covers several distinct property types, each with its own characteristics and price points:

**Terrace house** — the most accessible landed type, sharing party walls with adjacent units on one or both sides. Typically 3-4 storeys, built on plots of 150-250 sqm. Starting price in 2026: SGD 2.5-4M depending on location and tenure.

**Semi-detached house** — shares one party wall with one neighbour. More privacy and typically larger plots than terrace houses. Starting price: SGD 3.5-6M.

**Bungalow** — standalone house on its own plot, no shared walls. The segment that most buyers aspire to. Starting price: SGD 5-8M for non-GCB.

**Good Class Bungalow (GCB)** — the most prestigious landed category. <cite index="12-1">Minimum plot 1,400 sqm; 39 gazetted areas across Singapore.</cite> Price range: SGD 15M-50M+. The strictest eligibility restrictions apply here.

**Strata-landed (cluster housing/townhouses)** — a distinct category. <cite index="12-1">They are landed in appearance, each unit having its own ground floor and outdoor space, but are legally classified as strata units within a development, placing them outside the Residential Property Act's "restricted residential property" regime.</cite> This classification difference has significant eligibility implications — see below.

## Eligibility: Who Can Buy Landed Property in Singapore

This is where most buyers — and many agents — need to pay close attention.

### Singapore Citizens (SC)

<cite index="12-1">Singapore Citizens may purchase any landed residential property on the Singapore mainland — terrace, semi-detached, bungalow, or GCB — without any prior approval from the SLA or CRP.</cite> This is the broadest eligibility of any buyer group.

The only constraint for SC buyers is financial: stamp duty costs and mortgage eligibility determine what is affordable, not eligibility restrictions.

### Singapore Permanent Residents (PR)

PRs face significant restrictions on landed property. <cite index="17-1">PRs require approval from the Land Dealings Approval Unit (LDAU), the body under the Singapore Land Authority that vets landed purchases by non-citizens. Approval is assessed case-by-case and is not easy to get: in practice, you generally need to have been a PR for at least 5 years and be able to show an exceptional economic contribution to Singapore.</cite>

<cite index="17-1">Approved PRs are limited to landed properties of up to 15,000 sq ft outside Good Class Bungalow areas, and the home must be for your own occupation — you can't rent it out.</cite>

<cite index="18-1">Even with LDAU approval, a PR pays Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty at the PR rate.</cite>

**The practical reality:** LDAU approval is not guaranteed and the assessment process adds 30 working days minimum to the buying timeline. PRs who are serious about landed should apply for in-principle guidance from the LDAU before committing significant time to property search.

### Foreigners (Non-PR)

<cite index="12-1">Foreign nationals generally cannot purchase mainland landed property.</cite> The only practical exceptions are:

- **Strata-landed (cluster housing):** Generally purchasable by PRs and foreigners as these are classified as private residential rather than restricted property.
- **Sentosa Cove landed:** <cite index="17-1">Foreigners can buy a landed home with LDAU approval, on a plot of up to 1,800 sqm, strictly for owner-occupation.</cite>

<cite index="12-1">Good Class Bungalows: Reserved exclusively for Singapore Citizens — PRs and foreigners are excluded even with CRP or SLA approval.</cite>

### The Strata-Landed Alternative

For buyers who face eligibility constraints (PRs, some foreigners) or budget constraints, strata-landed deserves specific consideration. <cite index="12-1">Prices are typically lower than equivalent standalone landed: a cluster terrace in a popular development might list at SGD 2.5-4 million.</cite>

The trade-off: <cite index="12-1">strata-landed homes come with condominium management fees, are subject to the strata title's collective management and by-laws, and may carry restrictions on exterior modifications. The land beneath a strata-landed unit is held collectively, unlike the exclusive freehold or leasehold land title of a standalone landed property.</cite>

## The Cost Structure for Landed Property

### Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD)

BSD applies to all buyers on a progressive basis. The key difference for landed property is that the higher price quantum means the 5% and 6% BSD tiers apply to a significant portion of the purchase price.

For a SGD 5M bungalow:
- 1% on first SGD 180,000 = SGD 1,800
- 2% on next SGD 180,000 = SGD 3,600
- 3% on next SGD 640,000 = SGD 19,200
- 4% on next SGD 500,000 = SGD 20,000
- 5% on next SGD 1.5M = SGD 75,000
- 6% on remaining SGD 2M = SGD 120,000
- **Total BSD: approximately SGD 239,600**

### Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD)

For an SC buying their first private property after selling their HDB: 0% ABSD.

For an SC who still owns an HDB flat while purchasing: 20% ABSD (SGD 1M on a SGD 5M property), with the possibility of remission if the HDB is sold within 6 months.

For a PR: 5% ABSD on first private property.

### Financing Landed Property

<cite index="17-1">You can borrow up to 75% of the price or valuation, whichever is lower, for a first housing loan (tenure of 30 years or less, not running past age 65). That leaves a 25% downpayment — and on a landed quantum, that is a large sum.</cite>

For a SGD 5M landed purchase: 25% downpayment = SGD 1.25M (minimum SGD 250,000 of this must be cash; the remainder can be CPF or cash).

<cite index="17-1">Your loan is capped by TDSR. The Total Debt Servicing Ratio limits all your monthly debt repayments to 55% of your gross monthly income, stress-tested at an interest rate floor of 4%.</cite>

For a 75% loan on a SGD 5M property (SGD 3.75M loan) at a 30-year tenure, the monthly mortgage at 4% stress test rate is approximately SGD 17,900. To qualify under TDSR, the buyer's gross household income would need to exceed approximately SGD 32,500-33,000/month assuming no other outstanding debts.

## The Buying Process for Landed Property

### Step 1: Pre-purchase preparation

**For PRs:** Apply to the LDAU before serious property search. The application fee is <cite index="17-1">SGD 1,220 per property and the assessment takes around 30 working days.</cite> You can apply for in-principle approval before identifying a specific property.

**For all buyers:** Get an In-Principle Approval (IPA) from the bank. <cite index="17-1">This is a free assessment from a bank that confirms, based on your real income and debts, the loan amount you qualify for. Your IPA tells you what you can borrow and is valid for 30 to 90 days.</cite>

### Step 2: Property search

The landed market in Singapore is significantly less liquid than the condo market. New listings come to market less frequently, and many transactions occur off-market through agent networks before a property is formally listed. This makes agent relationships and network access more important in landed than in any other residential segment.

Key considerations when shortlisting:
- **Tenure:** Freehold vs leasehold matters significantly for landed — a 99-year leasehold bungalow with 40 years remaining has very different value dynamics from a freehold equivalent.
- **Land size and plot shape:** Plot size determines what you can rebuild/A&A (Additions and Alterations). A regular rectangular plot is preferable to an irregular shape.
- **Building setback requirements:** URA's Development Control regulations specify minimum setbacks from boundaries. These affect how much of the plot can be built on.
- **Drainage reserves and road lines:** Some landed plots have drainage reserves or road line reserves that reduce the effective usable land area. Always verify with the relevant authorities before committing.

### Step 3: OTP and completion

The Option to Purchase and completion process for landed property follows the same general structure as other private property purchases, with two key differences:

**Timeline:** <cite index="17-1">Plan for 3 to 6 months. If you need LDAU approval, that is about 30 working days on its own. Once you have exercised the OTP, the legal process and completion run roughly 8 to 12 weeks.</cite>

**Lawyers:** You will need a private conveyancing lawyer for the landed purchase. Unlike HDB transactions, there is no HDB Hub appointment — the legal process is handled entirely through your conveyancing lawyer and the seller's lawyer.

## What Agents Need to Know When Advising Landed Buyers

**Qualify eligibility before anything else.** Confirming the buyer's citizenship/PR status, the number of residential properties they currently hold, and their ABSD position should be the first conversation — before any property shortlisting begins.

**Understand the LDAU timeline for PR clients.** A PR client who has not yet started the LDAU process should not be looking at specific landed properties until they have received their approval. Building in 30+ working days for the LDAU assessment is non-negotiable.

**Know the GCB rules precisely.** <cite index="12-1">GCBs are reserved exclusively for Singapore Citizens.</cite> A PR client — regardless of how long they have been PR — cannot purchase a GCB.

**The strata-landed conversation.** Many buyers who aspire to landed lifestyle but face eligibility or budget constraints would benefit from understanding strata-landed as an alternative. Presenting this option clearly, with its trade-offs explained honestly, is a service most buyers do not receive from agents who are not specialist landed practitioners.

**Professional listing presentation matters more in landed.** A landed property is a significant asset, and buyers who find the property through your marketing will form their first impression from the listing presentation.
