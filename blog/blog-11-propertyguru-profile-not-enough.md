# Why Your PropertyGuru Profile Is Not Enough in 2026 (And What to Do Instead)

PropertyGuru dominates Singapore property search. With over 80% of buyers starting their property journey on the portal, it is an essential channel — and no serious agent should ignore it.

But "essential" is not the same as "sufficient." And in 2026, the agents who are building sustainable businesses — ones that grow through referrals and reputation rather than portal spend — are the ones who have built a presence beyond PropertyGuru.

Here is why your PropertyGuru profile alone is no longer enough, and exactly what to build instead.

---

## The Fundamental Problem With Portal-Only Presence

<cite index="16-1">The property industry in Singapore has shifted fundamentally. Buyers and sellers no longer walk into an agency and accept whichever agent is assigned. They research agents online, compare track records, read reviews, and choose someone whose personal brand resonates with them.</cite>

That research does not stop at PropertyGuru. When a seller is deciding which agent to list with, they will typically:

1. Find 3-4 agents through PropertyGuru listings in their area
2. **Google each agent's name** to see what comes up
3. Check LinkedIn for professional credibility
4. Look for reviews, testimonials, or any independent evidence of track record
5. Sometimes check Instagram or Facebook for personality fit

An agent who only exists on PropertyGuru passes step 1 and fails every subsequent step. The seller finds a profile page with transaction badges — identical to dozens of other agents — and no additional signal to differentiate.

<cite index="15-1">Agency-level marketing has its place, but it promotes the brand, not you. When a buyer searches for "property agent Punggol HDB" or "condo specialist Orchard Road," they want to find an individual agent who demonstrates expertise in that specific market. Your agency's website will not rank for those terms. Your personal marketing channels can.</cite>

---

## What PropertyGuru Cannot Do for You

Understanding the specific limitations of portal-only presence helps clarify what needs to be built elsewhere:

**PropertyGuru cannot rank for Google searches about you.** When someone Googles "Sarah Tan property agent Singapore," your PropertyGuru profile may appear — but it will be a generic portal page surrounded by PropertyGuru's own branding, not a page that showcases your specific expertise and personality.

**PropertyGuru cannot differentiate your listing presentation.** Every listing on PropertyGuru looks the same — same photo format, same description field, same contact button. There is no mechanism to make your listing look distinctly more professional than the one above it.

**PropertyGuru cannot build your referral reputation.** Referrals come from people who know you, trust you, and have seen evidence of your expertise. A portal profile does not build any of these — it is a transactional interface, not a relationship-building channel.

**PropertyGuru cannot follow up with buyers who leave.** Once a buyer exits your portal listing without enquiring, they are gone. You have no way to stay visible to them as they continue their property journey over the following weeks or months.

---

## What to Build Beyond PropertyGuru

### 1. A Professional Personal Website

<cite index="16-1">Build a personal website separate from your agency's site. This gives you complete control over your online presence and provides a permanent home for your listings, market insights, client testimonials, and contact information. A well-maintained personal website significantly enhances your credibility and SEO visibility.</cite>

Your personal website does not need to be elaborate. At minimum, it should include:
- Your professional profile and niche specialisation
- Your track record with specific transaction examples
- Client testimonials with specific outcomes
- A blog or market updates section (this is what builds Google visibility over time)
- A prominent WhatsApp CTA

The key difference from a portal profile: your personal website is entirely about you and controlled by you. The branding is yours. The content is yours. The lead capture is yours.

### 2. Dedicated Listing Pages for Each Property

A PropertyGuru listing is one of many on a search results page. A dedicated listing microsite is a standalone page — your branding, your property, your contact details as the exclusive focus.

When you share a mypropfolio listing link on WhatsApp or social media, the buyer arrives at a page that belongs entirely to that one property and to you as the presenting agent. There are no competing listings, no portal ads, no navigation away from your content.

<cite index="12-1">Agents who build strong online presence are often perceived as more experienced and reliable. Instead of chasing leads, clients start approaching them.</cite>

This perception shift happens when every touchpoint — from your Google search result to your WhatsApp sharing to your listing page — looks professional and consistent.

### 3. Active LinkedIn Presence

As covered in our LinkedIn guide, <cite index="13-1">fewer than 5% of Singapore property agents post actively on LinkedIn</cite> — which means the opportunity cost of not being there is enormous for agents targeting the upgrader, investor, and corporate relocation segments.

A Google search for your name that returns an active, well-maintained LinkedIn profile with consistent market content signals expertise immediately. A search that returns nothing but a portal profile signals that you exist, but says nothing about who you are.

### 4. Google Business Profile

This is the most underused free tool available to Singapore property agents.

A complete Google Business Profile means that when someone searches "[your name] property agent" or "HDB agent [your district]," a professional card appears in the search results — with your photo, your specialisation, your contact details, and eventually your reviews.

Setup takes 30 minutes and is entirely free. The ongoing maintenance is minimal: update your profile when you take on significant transactions, respond to any reviews you receive, and post occasional market updates.

### 5. Consistent Social Presence in Your Niche

<cite index="16-1">Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and YouTube provide agents with powerful tools to showcase properties, share market insights, and build relationships with potential buyers and sellers.</cite>

You do not need to be active on every platform. Pick one where your target audience concentrates:

- **HDB upgrader audience** → Facebook Groups for specific estates and condos
- **Investor/corporate audience** → LinkedIn
- **Younger buyer/renter audience** → Instagram Reels and TikTok
- **All audiences** → YouTube (property walkthrough videos have the longest content lifespan)

The key is consistency in a single niche, not sporadic presence across all platforms.

## The Compounding Advantage of Multi-Channel Presence

The agents who have built presence across multiple channels report a consistent pattern: the channels reinforce each other.

A seller who finds you on PropertyGuru, then Googles your name and finds your LinkedIn with active market content, then clicks your website and reads your transaction case studies — arrives at the listing appointment with a fundamentally different level of pre-existing trust than a seller who has only seen your portal profile.

<cite index="15-1">The agents who invest in real estate marketing systematically — rather than sporadically posting on social media — build compounding advantages.</cite>

Each channel you add does not just add linearly to your visibility — it multiplies the credibility signal from every other channel. A buyer who has seen your listing on PropertyGuru, received your mypropfolio listing link via WhatsApp, and seen your LinkedIn post about that specific area all in the same week is far more likely to arrange a viewing than a buyer who has only seen the portal listing.

---

## Where to Start

The most common mistake agents make when trying to build beyond PropertyGuru is trying to do everything at once. The result is mediocre presence everywhere rather than strong presence anywhere.

**Month 1:** Set up your Google Business Profile (free, 30 minutes) and create your first mypropfolio listing for your next property. These are the highest-leverage starting points.

**Month 2:** Optimise your LinkedIn profile and begin posting 3x per week. Focus on your niche area — transaction data, market commentary, personal stories.

**Month 3+:** Consider a personal website if you have not already — or use the mypropfolio platform as your agent hub while building out individual listing pages for every property you take on.

The agents who take this three-month approach consistently report stronger seller confidence at listing appointments and higher quality buyer enquiries within 6 months — because their multi-channel presence compounds over time.
