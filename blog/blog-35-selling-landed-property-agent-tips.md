# How to Market and Sell Landed Property in Singapore: Agent Tips (2026)

Marketing a landed property in Singapore is fundamentally different from marketing a condo or HDB flat — not because the core principles change, but because the buyer pool is smaller, the transaction quantum is higher, and the expectations around presentation, discretion, and agent expertise are substantially elevated.

An agent who applies the same approach to a SGD 6M bungalow as to a SGD 800K HDB flat will not get the same results. Here is how to approach landed differently.

---

## Understanding the Landed Buyer Pool

The first thing to internalise about the landed market is who can actually buy. <cite index="12-1">Singapore Citizens may purchase any landed residential property on the Singapore mainland without any prior approval. PRs require CRP approval. Foreign nationals generally cannot purchase mainland landed property.</cite>

This eligibility restriction has direct implications for how you price, market, and manage seller expectations:

**Smaller pool means longer average marketing periods.** A condo listing that might attract 20 serious enquiries in 4 weeks might attract 5-8 serious landed enquiries in 8-12 weeks. This is not a reflection of the property's quality — it is the structural reality of a more restricted market.

**HNW buyers research differently.** High-net-worth buyers in the SGD 4-10M+ range do not typically browse PropertyGuru the same way a first-time buyer searches for a condo. Many landed transactions originate from agent networks, private introductions, and off-market referrals rather than portal discovery.

**Family decision units are more complex.** A landed purchase at this quantum typically involves extended family input — spouse, adult children, parents in some cases. Managing the multiple stakeholders in the buyer decision is a more complex interpersonal dynamic than a young couple buying their first private property.

---

## Pricing Landed Property: What Makes It Different

Comparable analysis for landed property requires a more careful approach than HDB or condo pricing.

**Land value, not built area, drives the primary comparison.** For landed, the land size and land price per sqft is the primary valuation metric — the built-up area (built on the land) is secondary. A 3,000 sqft house on a 2,000 sqft plot in District 10 is priced very differently from a 3,000 sqft house on a 4,000 sqft plot in the same area, because the land value differential is the dominant factor.

**Tenure differences compound over time.** Freehold and 99-year leasehold landed properties in the same estate may appear similar on paper, but the 99-year leasehold property with 60 years remaining will transact at a meaningful discount. Buyers with a long investment horizon will pay the freehold premium; buyers who plan to hold 5-10 years may be more indifferent. Understanding the buyer's profile shapes how you position the tenure in your marketing.

**Renovation condition matters more than in condo.** A fully renovated versus original-condition bungalow can have a 20-30% price differential for similar land and built area. Unlike condos where buyers often prefer to renovate to their own taste, many landed buyers will pay a premium for a well-done renovation that is move-in ready.

**Comparable transactions are fewer.** There are significantly fewer landed comparable transactions per month than condo comparables in any given district. You may need to look back 6-12 months for comparables rather than the 60-90 days that is standard for condo pricing.

## How to Market a Landed Property

### Professional Photography and Drone Shots

The visual presentation of a landed property needs to convey several things simultaneously: the house itself, the garden/outdoor space (often the primary selling point), the surrounding estate, and the street context.

<cite index="23-1">For landed homes, aerial shots add real context. Note that drone flying in Singapore is regulated by CAAS and much of the island sits in restricted airspace, so permits and a licensed operator may be required. Build that into your timeline.</cite>

For any landed property, the photography package should include:
- Full interior walkthrough (every key room, wide-angle, professional lighting)
- Garden and outdoor entertaining areas (often the emotional hook for landed buyers)
- Aerial drone shots showing the plot size and surrounding neighbourhood context
- Street view showing the facade and driveway
- Any special features — private pool, large garage, heritage elements

Property photography for a SGD 5-8M landed home is not a place to economise. The visual first impression shapes whether HNW buyers will engage their agent to arrange a viewing — and at this price point, a mediocre photography set is a direct signal of how the listing is being managed.

### Listing Distribution: Portal vs Private Network

For landed property, the distribution strategy is different from mass-market condo marketing:

**Portal listings (PropertyGuru, 99.co):** Still necessary for discovery — some buyers and their agents will search portals even for landed. But portal reach is less dominant in landed than in condo, because the buyer pool is smaller and more networked.

**Agent network distribution:** This is where landed marketing differs most significantly. Circulating the listing among agents in your network who have active HNW buyer mandates — via WhatsApp broadcasts, agency internal listings, and direct agent-to-agent outreach — will generate the most qualified leads for a landed property.

**Dedicated listing page:** For a SGD 5-8M landed property, a mypropfolio listing page is particularly effective because it gives you a professional, comprehensive, shareable asset that can be circulated via WhatsApp to HNW buyer networks, used in listing appointment presentations, and sent to agents with relevant buyer mandates. The page can house the full photo gallery, drone footage link, site plan, floor plan, and your agent profile in a single clean presentation — appropriate for the level of the asset.

**Social media (LinkedIn, Instagram):** For landed at the upper end of the market, LinkedIn is particularly effective — the HNW buyer demographic for SGD 5M+ properties overlaps significantly with the senior professional and business owner profile that dominates LinkedIn.

## The Viewing Experience

Viewings for landed property follow different protocols from condo viewings.

**The seller or current occupant may need to be absent.** For condo viewings, the seller's presence is sometimes manageable. For a landed viewing, where buyers are evaluating whether they can see themselves and their family living there, having the current occupant present can inhibit honest feedback and emotional connection.

**Allow substantially more time.** A condo viewing typically takes 20-30 minutes. A landed viewing — covering a multi-storey house, garden, swimming pool if any, garage, and surrounding estate context — will take 45-90 minutes for a serious buyer. Build this into your scheduling.

**Provide a comprehensive pre-viewing information pack.** Serious landed buyers will want floor plans, site plans, land title details, tenure information, and any relevant property history (outstanding A&A works, URA written permission for any modifications) before or at the viewing. Have these prepared in advance.

**The estate walk:** For buyers who are unfamiliar with the landed estate, consider arriving 10 minutes early and doing a brief walk of the surrounding area before entering the property. This contextualises the neighbourhood character — which is a significant factor in landed purchase decisions — in a way that no listing page can replicate.

## Managing Seller Expectations

This is where landed listings most often go wrong. Sellers of landed property tend to have strong views about what their property is worth — often anchored on peak market prices from 2021-2022 or on anecdotal transaction data that may not represent accurate comparables.

**The data-first conversation:** Bring the actual comparable transactions to the listing appointment — land price per sqft, built area, renovation condition, tenure, and time on market for each comparable. Landed sellers respond better to specific data than to general market commentary.

**The extended timeline expectation:** Set a realistic marketing period expectation upfront. "For a property in this range, we should plan for 8-16 weeks to find the right buyer — not 4 weeks." A seller who is surprised by a 12-week marketing period will blame the agent; a seller who was told to expect it will hold their pricing conviction longer.

**Off-market first:** For premium landed properties where the seller values discretion, a quiet off-market phase (4-6 weeks circulating through agent networks before public portal listing) can be appropriate. This protects the seller's privacy and tests buyer appetite at the full asking price before committing to public visibility.

---

## Post-Offer Management

Landed transactions at the SGD 4M+ level typically involve more negotiation on price, terms, and renovation condition than standard condo transactions.

**Completion date flexibility:** Landed sellers often have more flexibility on completion timing — particularly if they are downsizing and have not yet identified their next property. Using completion date flexibility as a negotiating tool (rather than price) can help bridge price gaps.

**As-is condition clarification:** Unlike HDB transactions where the flat must be clean and clear of unauthorised works, landed transactions can involve negotiation over renovation condition, fixtures, and fittings. Get clear written agreement on exactly what is included and excluded before OTP exercise.

**Coordinating with lawyers early:** Given the higher transaction value and potential for complex title searches (drainage reserves, road line reserves, URA written permissions), engaging conveyancing lawyers on both sides early in the process prevents delays later.

## The Bottom Line

The landed market rewards agent preparation, network depth, and presentation quality more than any other residential segment. Buyers at this level expect their agent to know the market specifically, present the property professionally, and manage the transaction with discretion and competence.

The agents who consistently transact in the landed segment are not the ones who apply standard condo marketing to a landed listing. They are the ones who adapt their entire approach — from pricing methodology to photography brief to distribution strategy — to match the specific dynamics of this segment.
